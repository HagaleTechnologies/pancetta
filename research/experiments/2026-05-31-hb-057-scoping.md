## Diagnostic result — kill-switch passed by 3.86×

Run: `cargo run --release -p pancetta-research --example hb057_dt_history_potential`

Method: on the refreshed top-20 hard-200 WAVs (`main.json`
`per_wav_top_failures`), decode each WAV with production config
(multipass N=3 + V1 ON), then:

1. Build per-callsign DT history from baseline (jt9) truth sightings
   across the 20 WAVs. Restrict to callsigns with ≥2 distinct-WAV
   sightings (cross-WAV proxies for the cross-session rolling window
   pancetta would maintain in production).
2. Bucket each multi-WAV callsign by DT variance (max - min across
   sightings): stable (<0.1s), moderate (0.1–0.3s), unstable (>0.3s).
3. For every missed truth whose sender callsign falls in stable or
   moderate, check whether the truth's actual DT lies within ±0.2s of
   the callsign's leave-one-out median DT (recoverable-by-prior upper
   bound).

## Why the recoverable-by-prior count equals the target pop

It does not exceed it because the target pop is the *denominator* (we
only check recoverability for stable+moderate). It equals the target
pop because moderate variance is bounded at 0.3s, and the
leave-one-out median of a moderate-variance sighting sequence rarely
drifts more than 0.2s from any single sighting (median is exactly
robust to per-sample variance up to ~½ the spread). So a ±0.2s window
covers essentially the entire moderate+stable population by
construction. The recoverable count is therefore a **gate-coverage
upper bound**, not a recall-conversion estimate. Real recall lift in
Session 2 will depend on what LDPC + AP do with the narrowed sync
window — which is precisely the experiment.

## Decisions captured in the spec

1. **Storage: coordinator-level**, behind `Arc<RwLock<...>>`. The
   diagnostic's recovery population is defined by cross-WAV history; a
   per-decoder-thread design has zero access to it on fresh threads.
2. **Sync hook: localized_costas_sync_search + AP-path sync_search
   only.** Pass 1 (production full sync_search) is NEVER gated by DT
   prior — preserves new-station discovery. The prior accelerates
   *follow-up* in the residual passes.
3. **Window: max(0.2s, IQR × 3)** around per-callsign median; 0.2s
   floor matches the diagnostic; IQR×3 scales for moderate callsigns.
4. **History capacity: 10 sightings per callsign, 30-min eviction**
   (matches `pancetta-qso::callsign_continuity` window).
5. **Median + IQR (not mean + stddev)**: outlier robustness — JTDX
   rationale + the 38.4% unstable bucket shows the variance tail.
