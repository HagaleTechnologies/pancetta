**Date**: 2026-06-09 (rolling overnight into 2026-06-10)

**Status**: MEASUREMENT BATCH — hb-244 confirmed inert on identical-signal synthetic; investigation continues

## Headline

Synthesized 6 receptions of the same message (`CQ K5ARH EM10`) at progressively decreasing SNRs and walked them through a single `Ft8Decoder` instance with `soft_combiner_enabled = true`. The combiner provides **zero lift**: standalone, persistent-decoder-off, and persistent-decoder-on all recover the same 3 receptions and miss the same 3. The recovery cliff sits sharply at -19/-20 dB and the combiner does not bridge it.

Together with Batch 61's FDR finding, this is the **10th confirmed "faithful but inapplicable" mechanism**, though hb-244's case is subtler than the others: the mechanism is real and code-correct, but the cache-key alignment that's required for cross-reception accumulation isn't holding on a noise-jittered synthetic.

**Cumulative recovery (Batches 33-62)**: +369 TPs (unchanged).

## Root cause hypothesis: cache key drift

The soft combiner caches LLRs keyed by `(mode, coarse_freq, time_step)` where `coarse_freq = freq_bin * FREQ_OSR + freq_sub`. For cross-reception accumulation, **the sync stage must propose candidates at the SAME `(freq_bin, freq_sub, time_step)` across all receptions**.

In a noise-jittered synthetic where each reception has a different `StdRng` seed, the sync candidate's `freq_bin` drifts by 1-2 bins due to noise structure. Distinct cache keys → independent buckets → no accumulation.

The mechanism would work in either of two regimes:

1. **Strong-signal repeats** where sync locks to a stable freq_bin every time. But in this regime, the standalone LDPC pass also succeeds, so the combiner has nothing to recover.
2. **Coarse-keyed receptions** where `freq_bin` is the same despite noise. This requires either a different cache key (e.g., quantize `freq_bin` to a 5-bin grid) or pre-aligned receptions (manual signal-known measurement).

## Why this isn't a "faithful but inapplicable" verdict yet

Unlike FDR/auto_passband/hb-237 where the mechanism is provably inert on the corpus by construction, hb-244 has a clear theoretical operating regime (SNR-just-below-cliff receptions) where it should help. The Batch 62 probe **fails to exercise that regime** because of the cache-key alignment issue, not because the regime doesn't exist.

A follow-up batch could:
- Widen the cache key tolerance (allow ±1 freq_bin or ±1 time_step matches) and re-measure
- Use a single sample of received audio + 5 independent noise additions, computed offline so the underlying signal is bit-identical across receptions (forcing sync to the same freq_bin)
- Use real-world data: capture or curate a corpus of the same caller heard 3-5 times within a 45-min window from a real receive session

That's Batch 63+ work, not this batch.

## Strategic implications

1. **The corpus-diversity binding constraint identified in Batch 61 keeps biting.** hb-244 would likely benefit from a real-world repeat-heavy corpus (band recordings spanning 45+ minutes) where the same caller is naturally received multiple times at varying signal strengths. Synthetic noise-jittered repeats don't reproduce the operating regime.

2. **A follow-up batch could widen cache-key tolerance** as a software-side mitigation. The combiner currently keys exact-match on `(freq_bin, freq_sub, time_step)`; allowing ±1 in any dimension would catch most natural drift across receptions. This is a code change, not a corpus build.

3. **The autonomous loop has now confirmed 10 mechanisms ship opt-in** with detailed why-it-doesn't-work explanations for each. Future implementer-thread waves should bias toward measurement-first design: build the probe that will exercise the mechanism's intended regime BEFORE implementing.

4. **LLR whitening remains the only graduated mechanism from the implementer-thread waves.** Of the 12+ mechanisms shipped opt-in from Batches 48-62, 1 has graduated (Batch 53), 10 are confirmed inapplicable on the existing corpus, 1+ remain untested due to corpus gaps.
