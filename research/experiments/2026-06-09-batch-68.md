## Headline

User direction: "expand measurement first" before deciding hb-244 graduation. Batch 68 ran the 5-config hb-244 probe on:
- **hard_200** (200 slots, scatter across 41 days, low-repeat) with ft8_lib truth — non-repeat-heavy comparison
- **full 5/30 day** (2066 slots, 100% repeat-heavy by construction, same source as Batch 66's 500-slot subset) with ft8_lib truth

Results:

| Corpus | tol=0 Δ TPs | tol=0 Δ FPs | tol=1 Δ TPs | tol=1 Δ FPs |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| hard_200 (200 slots) | +0 | +5 | +1 | +24 |
| **repeat_heavy_530 (500 slots, Batch 66)** | **+8** | **+28** | +28 | +392 |
| **full 5/30 (2066 slots, Batch 68)** | **+0** | **+94** | **+82** | **+462** |

**The +8 TPs at tol=0 on the 500-slot sample is statistical noise.** At 2066 slots — same source, same regime, just larger — tol=0 produces ZERO new TPs.

tol=1 shows +82 TPs at 2066 slots but with 5.6× FP cost. Precision drops 0.6889 → 0.6835 (-0.5% absolute, -0.7% relative). That's a real precision drag.

**hb-244 stays default-OFF.** The corpus framework worked exactly as designed: expanded measurement caught what would have been a small-sample false positive before ship.

**Cumulative recovery (Batches 33-68)**: +369 TPs reported. hb-244 candidate withdrawn.

## What changed our mind

| Source | Verdict |
|---|---|
| Batch 62-63 synthetic | hb-244 inert |
| Batch 66 real-corpus 500-slot | hb-244 reverses: +8 TPs / +28 FPs |
| **Batch 68 real-corpus 2066-slot** | **+8 was sample noise: actually +0 TPs at scale** |

The 500-slot subsample was 24% of the full 5/30 day. The +8 TPs in that subsample didn't reflect a robust mechanism behavior — it reflected sample-to-sample variation in which slots got selected.

The full-day result is much more authoritative: 4.1× the sample size, 100% repeat-heavy by construction, same audio source. tol=0 gives +0 / +94. That's the real mechanism behavior on this corpus.

## Why tol=1 looks better but still doesn't graduate

| Tolerance | Δ TPs | Δ FPs | Ratio | Precision impact |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 0 | +0 | +94 | n/a | -0.001 |
| 1 | +82 | +462 | 0.18 | -0.005 |
| 2 | +71 | +608 | 0.12 | -0.008 |

Widening the cache-key tolerance produces more TPs but disproportionately more FPs. Even the best tolerance (1) has only 18% TP yield per FP cost.

Compare to LLR whitening (the only graduated mechanism, Batch 53): +4 TPs / **−713 FPs** on hard_1000. LLR whitening was precision-IMPROVING; hb-244 is precision-DRAGGING.

For an autonomous-station deployment that uses decodes to inform TX decisions, the precision drag is the operational cost. hb-244 doesn't pass the graduation bar.
