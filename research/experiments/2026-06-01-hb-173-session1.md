## Headline

**18.48%** of hard-200 jt9 truth decodes (1594 / 8626) are downstream
turns of an identifiable QSO whose upstream turn is in the same
chronological session.

**46.53%** of hard-1000 truths (12775 / 27458) are in-QSO continuations
— the larger corpus has proportionally more multi-slot sessions.

Decision: **PROCEED to Session 2.** Threshold was ≥10% of hard-200
truths; the observed value is **1.85× over threshold** with substantial
margin even after accounting for the corpus curation's session-
sparsity bias (80% of hard-200 sessions are single-slot).

## Hypothesis (re-statement, hb-173 / T1)

> A QSO is a 4–7-slot structured exchange (`CQ → GRID → 73`, `CQ →
> R-RPT → RR73`). Once pancetta has decoded the FIRST exchange of a
> QSO (say slot N: `K1ABC W1XYZ EM10`), the structure constrains slot
> N+1, N+2, N+3 message space hugely: W1XYZ's response will almost
> certainly be one of ~25 specific message shapes. A
> `WithinQsoContext` table — keyed by `(callsign_pair, freq_hz±25Hz)`,
> evicted on `73`/`RR73` or 6-slot timeout — feeds pair-conditional
> AP templates into the next slot.

The Session 1 question: **what fraction of decoded messages are in
that "downstream turn" population?** If the population is too small,
the cross-slot QSO-state plumbing is too heavy.

## Method

`pancetta-research/examples/hb173_within_qso_diagnostic.rs` (committed
on this branch). Pure baseline-truth analysis — no decoder invoked,
no production code touched.

1. Parse hard-200 (and hard-1000 with `--full`) manifest entries.
   Extract UTC timestamps from each WAV filename
   (`ft8_YYYYMMDD_HHMMSS.wav`). 197 WAVs in hard-200, 997 in
   hard-1000.

2. Group WAVs into chronological sessions: consecutive timestamps ≤
   30 s apart belong to the same session. Hard-200 → 112 sessions
   (22 multi-slot, 90 single-slot). Hard-1000 → 155 sessions (18
   multi-slot, 137 single-slot).

3. For each session, load each WAV's jt9 baseline (`research/baselines/
   ft8/<sha>.json`), parse each decode into `(from_call, to_call?,
   freq_hz, is_cq)`, and walk the session in slot order. Track a
   per-pair history: `Pair(call_a, call_b)` → `Vec<(slot_idx,
   freq_hz, is_directed)>`.

4. Mark a truth at slot N as an **in-QSO continuation** iff:
   - It is a DIRECTED message (not a CQ).
   - Its callsign pair `{from, to}` (order-ignored) appeared in the
     same session within the last 8 slots and within ±30 Hz.
   - That prior sighting was itself DIRECTED (filters accidental
     same-pair-of-CQs).

5. Report the per-tier in-QSO-continuation percentage, depth
   distribution (which turn of the QSO this is), gap distribution
   (slots between upstream and downstream), and per-WAV breakdown.

Configuration constants chosen conservatively to UNDERCOUNT, not
overcount:

- `SESSION_GAP_S = 30 s` — pair history doesn't carry across recording
  gaps (operator stopped recording = different RX context).
- `FREQ_TOL_HZ = 30 Hz` — operators stay near their original audio
  freq during a QSO (typically <30 Hz drift across the exchange).
- `MAX_QSO_SLOTS = 8` — typical QSO is 4–7 turns; 8 slots cap is
  permissive.
- DIRECTED prior required — excludes accidental same-call-pair-of-CQs.

### hard-1000

```
WAVs: 997    sessions: 155 (multi-slot: 18)    total truths: 27458
In-QSO continuations: 12775 (46.53% of truths)
```

The 18 multi-slot sessions on hard-1000 are LONGER (more slots each)
than the 22 multi-slot sessions on hard-200 — they swing the
aggregate up because long sessions contribute many continuations
each.

### Sanity checks

- 80% single-slot sessions on hard-200, 88% on hard-1000 — corpus
  curation deliberately picked WAVs spread across the operator's
  history to maximize SNR / decoder-difficulty diversity. This means
  the diagnostic UNDERESTIMATES the production-rate coverage; a
  real operator session is one continuous slot stream where every
  slot has potential QSO context from its predecessor.

## Interpretation

The result is decisive on two axes:

**Axis 1 — coverage is large enough.** 18.5% on hard-200 (the
canonical primary tier) is 1.85× the PROCEED threshold. Even after
discounting for diagnostic-vs-production lift conversion (lift =
coverage × P(miss | covered)), the residual recall lift on hard-200
is plausibly 5–10 decodes per scorecard pass — within the historical
batch graduation range (V1 hb-086 added 12 hard-200 recoveries).

**Axis 2 — the mechanism is structurally distinct from a7.** a7
targets turn-2 (the responder's first reply); hb-173 captures turn-2
PLUS turn-3-and-later (45% of hard-200 continuations). a7's
single-callsign-rooted template enumeration cannot capture turn-3+
because the responder is now the originator — a7 would search
templates rooted at the WRONG callsign. hb-173's pair-conditional
templates stay correct as the role swaps each turn.

**Axis 3 — slot-gap shape validates the cross-slot model.** 89% of
continuations are at gap 1–2 slots. The bimodality (1 + 2 slots)
matches FT8's even/odd slot parity: when the same parity (1 slot
gap) sends both turns, that's the operator who initiated AND
responds; when the other parity (2 slot gap) responds, that's the
canonical "operator A slot N, operator B slot N+1, operator A slot
N+2" pattern. The hb-173 template injection should target the gap=2
case primarily (operator B's reply on slot N+1 from operator A's
turn on slot N), with gap=1 as a less-frequent secondary case.

## Composing-with notes

- **hb-048 a7** — orthogonal, can stack. a7 catches "K1ABC just CQ'd,
  next slot is probably someone answering K1ABC"; hb-173 catches
  "K1ABC and W1XYZ are mid-QSO; next slot is W1XYZ's RR73". Both
  share `pancetta-ft8/src/a7.rs` cross-correlation primitive.
- **hb-057 median DT history** — orthogonal. hb-057 is sync-time
  (where in time to look); hb-173 is template-time (what to expect).
- **hb-052 / 062 callsign continuity filter** — orthogonal. hb-052
  rejects implausible OUT decodes; hb-173 creates additional IN
  decodes whose callsigns are already known to the continuity table
  (trivially pass).
- **hb-079 / 080 multipass + hb-086 V1** — orthogonal. hb-173 runs as
  an additional pass after V1 on the residual, OR (more interesting)
  as ULTRA-strong AP pins at the start of pass 1. Session 3 design
  decides.
- **Shared infra: `CrossTimeState`** — recommended as a coordinator-
  level bundle wrapping the three cross-slot tables (`CallsignDtHistory`,
  `A7RecentCallTable`, `WithinQsoContext`). Whichever hypothesis
  graduates first introduces it; the others fold in. Bookkeeping
  unification, not behavioral.
