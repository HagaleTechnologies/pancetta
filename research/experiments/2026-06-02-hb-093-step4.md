# hb-093 step-4 — extend residual SNR gate to coherent_subtract_and_repass step 4 (SHELVED)

**Date**: 2026-06-02
**Branch**: iter/2026-06-02-hb-093-step4
**Status**: SHELVED — no threshold simultaneously satisfies the elapsed-≥10%
and recall-CI⊂[-1,+1] criteria. Step-4 candidates have already passed
the residual `costas_sync_search`, so their per-position SNR distribution
is shifted high enough that aggressive gates kill real decodes
(-115 rec at -3 dB, -45 at -5 dB, -10 at -7 dB), and the only safe
threshold (-10 dB) fires too rarely to give meaningful elapsed savings.

## Hypothesis

Per the hb-093 Phase A journal (2026-06-01): the residual SNR gate at
`joint_pair_retry_pass` worked as designed (filtered 37% of candidates
at -5 dB with 0% decode loss in the diagnostic), but joint_pair_retry's
slice of total elapsed was too small (~2-3% ceiling, far below the 10%
graduation bar).

The recommended extension: apply the same gate to
`coherent_subtract_and_repass` step 4 (the post-sync-search decode loop
on the residual). The per-WAV candidate count there is bounded by
`max_sync_candidates` (=200) — typically ~10× larger than
joint_pair_retry's ~10-30. Same threshold sweep, same kill switch.

## Implementation

`pancetta-ft8/src/decoder.rs`:

1. Mirrored the joint_pair_retry_pass gate inline in step 4 of
   `coherent_subtract_and_repass`: after `par_extract_symbols_from_spectrogram`
   and `par_estimate_snr_spectrogram`, skip the candidate (and record in
   the diagnostic stream when enabled) if `pre_snr_db < threshold`.
2. Changed `coherent_subtract_and_repass(&self, …)` → `&mut self` so the
   diagnostic path can push to `residual_snr_records`. Only one caller;
   no API ripple.
3. Updated doc comments on `Ft8Config::residual_snr_gate_db` and
   `residual_snr_diagnostic` to note the gate now fires at BOTH
   `joint_pair_retry_pass` AND `coherent_subtract_and_repass` step 4.

## Decision: SHELVE

Graduation criteria per the iter spec:

1. **Elapsed**: drops ≥10% under like-for-like conditions.
2. **Recall**: 95% CI ⊂ [-1, +1] (zero loss).

No setting satisfies BOTH:

- gate=−3 / −5 / −7 dB: produce meaningful elapsed reduction in the
  low-contention runs (m3: −39.7%, m5: −13.6%) but FAIL the recall
  bracket badly — the lower bounds of the CIs sit at −139 / −57 / −16
  decodes respectively, none of which is within [-1, +1].
- gate=−10 dB: PASSES the recall criterion (CI [0, 0], 0 lost decodes
  out of 4942) but the gate fires too rarely at this threshold to give
  meaningful elapsed savings. The contention-inflated m10 number is not
  interpretable for the elapsed criterion.

The plumbing is preserved at `residual_snr_gate_db: None` (default off);
the gate now applies at BOTH `joint_pair_retry_pass` AND step 4 when set,
so a future revisit (combining with hb-016 residual-energy stop or
different mechanism) doesn't need to re-implement.

## Lessons

1. **Step-4 candidate SNR is shifted higher than joint_pair_retry.**
   Step-4 candidates have already passed the residual
   `costas_sync_search` (sync_score ≥ residual_min_sync_score). Their
   per-position SNR distribution is consequently above the
   "noise-candidate" range that the joint_pair_retry diagnostic showed
   could be cheaply filtered. The decoded-SNR floor for step-4 sits
   between −10 and −7 dB (since the gate at −7 still costs 10 decodes
   while −10 costs zero) — much higher than the −3.7 dB joint_pair_retry
   floor measured in the Phase A diagnostic.

2. **Recall-zero-loss and elapsed-savings are in tension at this site.**
   The mechanism (cheap SNR proxy gating the LDPC pass) works
   correctly. But the SNR distribution at step-4 means the "safe"
   threshold (−10 dB) leaves almost no work to skip, and any threshold
   that skips meaningful work (≤−7 dB) takes a real recall hit.

3. **Concurrent agent CPU contention contaminates elapsed measurement
   for short runs.** The m7 and m10 evals coincided with another
   `chrono-replay` agent saturating the same 10 cores. Their elapsed
   numbers (+16.6%, +37.9% vs baseline) are not interpretable for the
   gate's CPU cost — they're host load, not gate behavior. Future iters
   that depend on absolute elapsed measurement should either gate on
   `nproc`-free windows or compare paired runs spaced milliseconds apart
   within a single process.

4. **The hb-093 mechanism is fundamentally lost-cause at this level of
   the decoder.** Two SHELVEs (Phase A: pair_retry slice too small;
   Phase B step-4: candidate SNR distribution too high) suggest the
   "skip LDPC at noise-only positions" play has been mined out. Future
   wall-clock optimization should look at a different lever (e.g.
   hb-016 residual-energy stop tuning, or LDPC iter cap by SNR).
