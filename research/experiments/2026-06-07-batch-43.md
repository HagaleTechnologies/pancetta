## Detail: hb-221 re-measurement

Batch 42 claimed +118 TPs for 5-window sliding decode. The probe's
baseline was a 12.64s-truncated single decode (4724 TPs). When
re-measured against the TRUE production baseline (15s full WAV
passed in single decode_window call, 5301 TPs):

- Production gets `num_blocks = 180000/1920 = 93`, `num_steps = 186`,
  `max_time_step = 27` — scans 28 internal Costas t0 positions
  covering dt ∈ [0, 2.16s].
- Truncated 12.64s window gets `num_blocks = 79`, `num_steps = 158`,
  `max_time_step = 0` — searches ONLY t0=0.
- 2-window {0, 2.0} thus covers 2 discrete dt positions vs production's
  28 continuous positions. Union loses to production.

3+ windows cover more dt range than 2 windows, eventually beating
production. But the precision cost (5-window precision 62.5% vs
production 74.6%) means the gain (+102 TPs at 5x decode cost) needs
downstream FP filters to be operationally viable.

**Decision**: defer ship. Update hb-221 priority 0.55 → 0.40.
Coordinator integration is non-trivial; the +29 TP / 3-window option
may not justify the cost. Re-evaluate once hb-228 (JTDX 3-method
spectral sweep) ships — that may compose better.

## Research highlights

### JTDX agent (3 new bank entries promoted)

JTDX has 3 mechanisms NOT in pancetta:

1. **hb-228 — 3-method spectral sweep**. JTDX runs Costas sync on
   3 different magnitude maps (sqrt/power/L1) from the SAME FFT, each
   with different syncmin. Different signals pop under different
   metrics. **Genuinely orthogonal to mp=2** (which is residual-
   based). Per agent: "Largest expected recall lift." ~1 day port.
   Highest-priority Batch 44 ship candidate.

2. **hb-229 — QSO partner band-collapse**. JTDX narrows search to
   ±60 Hz around active partner freq during in-flight QSO. Pure
   CPU/operational win, ~0 TP recall. Plumbing exists via hb-091
   freq_bin_range.

3. **hb-230 — relaxed sync near partner freq**. Even without
   band-collapse, JTDX uses syncmin=1.1 within ±3 Hz of partner.
   Small TP win for weak partner messages.

Anti-recommendations from JTDX:
- DT-distance thinning (would HURT pancetta's slot-edge hole from Batch 40)
- napwid AP gating (pancetta's AP not currently a FP source)
