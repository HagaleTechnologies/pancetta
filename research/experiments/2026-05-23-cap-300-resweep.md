## Hypothesis

shifted production to nms-off, which lets pass 1 see more candidates.

on full 5-tier and decide if the gain is worth the wall-clock cost.

## Disposition

**WIN.** Production `MAX_SYNC_CANDIDATES` const raised 200 → 300.
Composite +0.0023, no regressions, sensitivity gain meaningfully
larger than the +0.0008 from hb-005 or +0.0003 from hb-006. The
wall-clock cost is significant but operationally acceptable.

## Learnings

- **The elbow shifted with NMS off.** The hb-003 sweep set cap=200
  under nms-on (graduated 2026-05-22). nms-off (hb-019) made pass 1
  see more candidates, which pushed the elbow further out. Lesson:
  parameter elbows established under one production state may not
  hold under another. After every WIN that flips a structural knob,
  the adjacent parameter sweeps are worth re-running.

- **Speed/sensitivity is a tradeoff curve, not a frontier.** hb-031
  cut wall-clock 50% at -0.0007 composite. hb-038 trades half of
  that back for +0.0023 composite. Net since pre-experiment-run:
  composite +0.0591, wall-clock still ~25% better than the
  baseline (was 848s on a 5-tier, now ~1211s but with more decodes
  recovered). The right-shaped knob would be a runtime mode
  (`latency` vs `balanced` vs `deep`), but flat defaults serve until
  on-air feedback says otherwise.

- **Diminishing returns on parameter sweeps continue but aren't
  exhausted.** This +0.0023 is bigger than the recent marginal wins
  (hb-005 +0.0008, hb-006 +0.0003). Periodic re-sweeps after
  structural changes (like hb-019) can resurface modest wins.
