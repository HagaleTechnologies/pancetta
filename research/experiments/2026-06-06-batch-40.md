**64% of isolated strong-misses are at slot-edges**: 26% pre-edge

This is the structural finding. Compared to the corpus overall
(Batch 36 C2: ~16% of hard-200 truths at dt<0; less at dt≥2.0), the
isolated strong-miss population is HEAVILY concentrated at slot-edges
— exactly where pancetta's Costas sync range cuts off.

### Middle bucket (dt 0-2s, isolated): 130 truths (36%)

These are NOT slot-edge. They're isolated, strong, in the middle of
the slot, and pancetta still missed them. This is a sub-population
worth separate diagnostic in a future batch:

- Could be LDPC convergence failures (sync passes but parity errors)
- Could be CRC failures with high error rate
- Could be AGC/normalization issues
- Could be specific signal-quality issues (frequency drift, doppler)

Subset for hb-220 follow-on diagnostic in Batch 41+.

## hb-220 — spawned

Slot-edge sync expansion via coordinator-level audio buffering. Plan-
sized (3-4 sessions). Headroom: realistic 150-200 isolated TPs (227
slot-edge isolated + likely some overlap with capture-locked slot-edge
from Batch 36 C2's 83-truth dt<-0.5 cliff).

Mechanism:
1. Coordinator buffers ~2s of prior-slot audio for early-edge cases
2. Decoder builds extended spectrogram from concatenated audio
3. Costas sync iterates t0 from `-pad_steps` to `max_time_step + pad_steps`
4. Decode pipeline unchanged

Risk: coordinator-side buffering introduces stateful decoding, which
complicates the rolling-window AP context (hb-050) and chrono-replay
tier. Needs careful architectural design.
