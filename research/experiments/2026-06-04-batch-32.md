## Production wires

### 1. hb-103 content-score wire in autonomous TX

`pancetta-qso/src/autonomous.rs`:
- `DecodedMessageInfo` gains `confidence: Option<f32>` and `time_offset_s: Option<f64>`
- `CqCandidate` gains `message_text: String`, `confidence: Option<f32>`, `time_offset_s: Option<f64>`
- `update_decodes()` plumbs the new fields from `DecodedMessageInfo`
- `decide()` CQ-respond path adds a content-score gate at
  **`SHIP_CONSERVATIVE` (+0.35)**, not `SHIP_PRECISE` (+2.977)

**Why CONSERVATIVE, not PRECISE**: a CQ message is single-callsign
("CQ K1ABC FN42"), so the `in_trust_set_both` bonus (+2) structurally
can't fire. The max achievable score for ANY single-callsign CQ is
≈ 2.5 (max trust_any + max confidence + best dt + best SNR), well
below `SHIP_PRECISE`. `SHIP_PRECISE` was calibrated on the full
hard-200 mix that includes 2-callsign exchanges (where `both` fires);
applying it as the CQ gate would block ALL legitimate CQs.

`SHIP_CONSERVATIVE` (+0.35) is the right threshold for autonomous TX:
100% recall on the hard-200 corpus (Diagnostic T) while eliminating
34% of FPs. Weak/spurious CQs (low confidence, late dt, low SNR) fall
below the threshold.

**Plumbing change**: `pancetta/src/coordinator/autonomous.rs:468`
populates the new `confidence` and `time_offset_s` fields from
`DecodedMessage`.

**Tests**:
- `test_content_score_blocks_low_score_cq_at_autonomous_tx` — verifies
  low-confidence + late-dt CQ blocked even when callsign is trusted
- `test_content_score_permits_high_score_cq_at_autonomous_tx` — verifies
  high-confidence trusted CQ passes the gate

Plus 10 existing test fixtures and 7 loopback-test fixtures updated
to include `confidence: None, time_offset_s: None` (Option-shaped so
absent values pass through — preserves all pre-batch test semantics).

All workspace tests green.

### W — logistic regression

Trained a batch logistic regression (gradient descent, 2000 iters,
lr=0.05) on the 6975-sample dataset with 7 features including the new
`decode_time_s`.

Result: **LR converges to AUC 0.874**, slightly lower than the
hand-weighted 0.886. The LR optimizes log-loss (calibration), not AUC,
and under 30% class imbalance it diverges from AUC-optimal.

Notably, the learned weights include `-0.80 * decode_time_s` —
validating that decode time is a meaningful signal. The hand-weighted
formula doesn't yet include it; a future hb-103 v2 should add this
feature with a similar weight.

Other learned-weight insights:
- `in_trust_set_both` weight is +2.7 (hand had +2.0) — slightly under-weighted
- `in_trust_set_any` weight is +1.6 (hand had +1.0) — significantly under-weighted
- `confidence` weight is -0.39 (LR is partitioning attribution across
  correlated features; not a real disagreement with the AUC=0.706
  per-feature finding)

For shipping decision: hand-weighting holds. The decode_time_s addition
+ tighter trust weights would lift AUC by maybe +0.01–0.02. Not worth
re-shipping in this batch but documented for hb-103 v2.

### X — characterize 31.3% surviving FPs at SHIP_PRECISE

Of 2040 FPs in the test corpus, 639 (31.3%) survive the
`SHIP_PRECISE` threshold even after callsign-continuity + pattern
rejects. Their profile:

| Slice | Count | % of survivors |
|---|---:|---:|
| confidence ≥ 0.9 | 418 | 65% |
| dt < 3.0s (TP-like timing) | 450 | 70% |
| dt 3-8s | 183 | 29% |
| dt ≥ 8.0s | 6 | 1% |

The survivors are **TP-shaped**: high confidence, TP-like timing.
Looking at the top-30 by score:
- Multiple repeats of the same text (`W3WDK WZ9B EM58` appears 3×,
  `WY7WL KJ4VIQ FM14` appears 3×) across different WAVs — these are
  likely **jt9-missed real signals** that pancetta recovered (hb-024
  cross-validation pattern: pancetta's "novels" are often real)
- Real-callsign 2-pair forms throughout, with trusted callsigns
- All have confidence = 1.00 (the decoder is confident)

The classifier is correctly NOT rejecting these. Most "FPs" at this
level are jt9-baseline gaps, not actual noise FPs.

Implication: hb-103's headroom isn't much larger than the
current operating point on this corpus. The next attack surface isn't
better filtering — it's better truth (jt9 has gaps) or a different
mechanism (adding decode_time_s could push 1-2% more, but the gains
are diminishing).

## Substance-check notes

- **Threshold-tuning insight matters more than weight-tuning**. The
  most impactful change in this batch was recognizing SHIP_PRECISE
  doesn't fit CQs and using SHIP_CONSERVATIVE for autonomous TX. The
  LR results (and lack of improvement over hand-weights) suggest the
  weights are already near-optimal; the bigger gains came from
  threshold selection.
- **decode_time_s is the next obvious feature** for hb-103 v2 — AUC
  0.695, LR-learned weight -0.80. Not shipped this batch because:
  (a) feature already 100% populated, (b) plumbing through
  DecodedMessageInfo would extend the existing 2-field plumbing,
  (c) hand-weighted v1 AUC 0.886 is already strong.
- **The hb-024 cross-validation effect resurfaced** — many "FPs"
  surviving the score are actually jt9-baseline gaps. The classifier
  ceiling on this corpus is constrained by truth quality, not score
  quality.
- **DXpedition reject is operator-corpus-specific.** K5ARH's hard-200
  has no active DXpeditions. A different operator on an
  expedition-rich corpus would have different numbers. Documented as
  a future opt-in config.
- **The 1 TP lost in the is_plausible change is acceptable**. If
  it's a real DXpedition or free-text, it's in the noise floor of
  what the autonomous-station profile values.
