## What ships

**Single line change** in `Ft8Config::default()`:

```rust
osd_depth: Some(2),  // OLD
osd_depth: Some(0),  // NEW (Batch 73)
```

Why `Some(0)` not `None`:
- Both have identical TPs/precision measurements (within 1 TP / 1 decode)
- Some(0) preserves the OSD-0 single-trial path (zero-cost in measurement; preserves architectural surface for edge cases)
- None skips OSD machinery entirely (slightly faster but disturbs the OSD-as-code-path contract)

Conservative ship: `Some(0)`.

## Why hard_200's defaults didn't generalize

hard_200 (200 hand-picked hard-to-decode slots spanning 41 days) was the original tuning corpus. It biased toward "deep OSD recovers more TPs":
- The hand-curated hard slots benefit more from OSD-2's flip search
- But the price of producing thousands of spurious FPs on the bulk of normal traffic was never measured on a realistic corpus

The corpus framework (Batch 65) made this measurement possible. Without it, the default would have stayed Some(2) indefinitely.

## Strategic implications

1. **The hard_200/hard_1000-tuned defaults need a broader audit.** OSD depth was the most-obviously-wrong knob. Others (ldpc_iterations, max_decode_passes, max_sync_candidates, max_parity_errors_for_osd) may have similar issues.

2. **Future tuning should be cross-corpus by default.** Single-corpus optima are visibly misleading. The Batch 72 pattern (one-corpus finding → cross-corpus verify before ship) should be the standard.

3. **The autonomous loop's productive yield was NOT exhausted.** Batch 71's "no more ideas" framing was wrong. There are still:
   - 8 untested shelved mechanisms (per user iteration plan #2)
   - Cross-day callsign trust (per user iteration plan #3)
   - 11 remaining B53-64 finding re-verifies (per user iteration plan #4)
   - More knob sweeps (post-Batch-72, the obvious next sweep is max_sync_candidates × max_parity_errors_for_osd)
