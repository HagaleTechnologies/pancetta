subtraction doesn't contain decodable signal at lower thresholds.
The remaining undecoded population (40% miss rate on hard-200) needs
DIFFERENT mechanisms — not threshold/iteration knob flips.

## Multi-interval probe — the headline win

**Mechanism**: pancetta's `decode_window` requires ≥12.64s of audio
(151,680 samples) but the hard-200 WAVs are 15s (180k samples). The
internal Costas sync iterates t0 from 0 to 27 time-steps (each 80ms),
covering signal start times dt ∈ [0, 2.16s]. **Signals at dt > 2.16s
or dt < 0 are unreachable in a single decode.**

The multi-interval probe runs decode at multiple audio offsets:
- offset 0.0: samples[0..151680] — covers dt ∈ [0, 0]
- offset 0.5: samples[6000..157680] — covers dt ∈ [-0.5, ~0]
- offset 1.0: samples[12000..163680] — covers dt ∈ [-1.0, ~-0.5]
- offset 1.5: samples[18000..169680] — covers dt ∈ [-1.5, ~-1.0]
- offset 2.0: samples[24000..175680] — covers dt ∈ [-2.0, ~-1.5]

(Each offset's Costas sync iterates t0 from 0 to ~0; with 12.64s
audio there's no room for time search inside the window. The offset
acts as a time shift.)

| start_s | decodes | TPs | precision |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 0.00 | 5671 | 4724 | 83.3% |
| 0.50 | 5380 | 4421 | 82.2% |
| 1.00 | 5171 | 4235 | 81.9% |
| 1.50 | 5182 | 4266 | 82.3% |
| 2.00 | 4639 | 3714 | 80.1% |
| **UNION** | **8646** | **5403** | 62.5% |
| **prod baseline (15s window)** | 7082 | 5285 | 74.6% |
| **Δ union vs prod** | +1564 | **+118** | -12.1pp |

Each 12.64s window individually decodes LESS than the production 15s
window (because production's 15s gives it 28 time-search positions
internally; my truncated 12.64s gives it 0-1 positions). But the UNION
across 5 offsets exceeds production by +118 TPs.

**Mechanism**: different audio start positions surface different
Costas time-search candidates, especially **late-dt signals** that
production can't reach (signal dt > 2.16s). hb-220 covers the negative-
dt component; hb-221 (this) covers the late-dt component, with some
overlap.

**Cost**: 5x decode operations. Multi-window union is precision-cheap
in absolute terms (62.5% vs 74.6%) because each window adds candidates
that are real TPs at one offset but not seen at others. Production's
internal scan already merges across t0 positions; multi-window just
extends the reachable dt range.

**Promoted to hb-221** (priority 0.55, 1-2 sessions for 2-window
variant). Bank entry documents the implementation choices.

## ft8mon research — 6 promoted to bank

Agent surveyed ft8mon (rtmrtmrtmrtm/ft8mon, AB1HL) at the source-code
level. Returned implementation-ready findings:

### hb-222 — `search_both_known` post-decode subtraction refinement
After successful LDPC+CRC decode, re-optimize (Hz, time-offset) to where
the known 79-symbol pattern best matches via the `known_strength_how=7`
score (negative-of-sum-of-magnitudes-of-symbol-FFT-bin-differences). Then
subtract using that refined position. **Multipass amplifier**: cleaner
subtraction → cleaner residual → more decodes from residual. ~250 LOC.

### hb-223 — `soft_decode_pairs` independent LLR producer
Evaluate |csum| = |m79[si][s1] + m79[si+1][s2]| for all 64 (s1,s2)
pairs of adjacent symbols. Use max-correlation pair to fix bit
probabilities. Coherent integration over 2 symbols recovers ~1-3 dB
on flat-fading channels (literature). Runs independently from
single-symbol soft decode; whichever LDPC-decodes first wins. ~150 LOC.

### hb-224 — `osd_ldpc_thresh=70` gate
Only invoke OSD when BP terminates with ≥70/83 parity checks correct
(0.843 fraction). Skips OSD when BP is hopeless OR already won.
~5-20% CPU savings on multipass paths; could fund higher osd_depth.
~30 LOC.

### hb-225 — 2-D coarse sub-bin Costas grid
4×4 = 16 sub-bin offsets per FFT bin during coarse Costas search via
cached global FFT + bin-rotation + IFFT trick (not 16 fresh FFTs).
Targets scalloping loss in band-middle 1000-2000 Hz (Batch 34
finding). ~250 LOC.

### hb-226 — Inter-symbol phase-bridged subtraction reconstruction
Reconstruct decoded signal for subtraction with Gaussian-ramp inter-
symbol transitions matching GFSK modulator. Read next symbol's measured
phase, compute correction to bridge actual→target, spread linearly
across ramp samples. Pairs with hb-222 — both multipass amplifiers.
~200 LOC.

### hb-227 — `apriori174[]` empirical bit-frequency prior
174-element array of empirical P(bit=1) frequencies from statistical
analysis of correct codewords. Applied as Bayesian prior to initial
LLR. Requires corpus extraction from pancetta's decodes (don't lift
ft8mon's table — bit ordering may differ). ~30 LOC implementation +
1 session corpus extraction.

## Process learnings

1. **Probe baselines matter a lot**. The Batch 41 ldpc=200 ship
   absorbed a +4 TP from DC offset removal that would have shown up in
   a separate batch. When sweeping config knobs, the baseline must be
   current production state.

2. **Web research is high-leverage**. The ft8mon agent's findings
   would have taken many batches to discover internally. Cost was one
   research-agent session; payoff is 6 ranked bank entries with
   citations. Continue investing here.

3. **Null findings are valuable**. Batch 42's 14-variant sweep killed
   8 backlog items and confirmed hb-086 V3's shelve generalizes to
   the global sync-threshold case. Future batches won't waste sessions
   on these.

4. **Parametric sweeps need an `N` cap**. The full hard-200 sweep took
   2.5 hours wall-clock with multi-interval running in parallel. A
   `BATCH42_N=50` subset would have given directional answers in 30
   min, full validation could follow on a winning variant.

5. **The ideation/exploitation pattern works**. Per the user's reframe
   (auto-research style), this batch shipped ZERO new TPs but produced
   a rich backlog (7 new bank entries) + a measured +118-TP candidate
   ready to ship next batch. The character of "discovery batch" vs
   "ship batch" alternates naturally.
