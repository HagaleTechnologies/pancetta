## Diagnostic Z + AA + BB + CC (one decode pass)

Single binary covers four characterizations.

### Z — strong-signal recall

Full hard-200, jt9 truths at SNR ≥ -10 dB:
- 4860 strong truths total
- 3284 recovered by pancetta
- **1576 missed (32.4% miss rate)**

The 75.6% recall from Batch 30 O was on top-20 lid_of_band — a
narrower slice. Full hard-200 is harder. 67.6% strong-signal recall is
the production baseline.

### AA — capture-effect lockdown

For each missed strong truth, check if another truth sits within ±25 Hz
(the capture-effect boundary from Batch 29 J) and ±1 s on the same WAV.

**Result: 1058 of 1576 misses (67.1%) are capture-locked.**

Two-thirds of strong-signal misses occur because another signal within
25 Hz of the same slot blocks both. The capture-effect dominates
strong-signal recall failures. This is the structural mechanism Batch 29 J
identified, now quantified at corpus scale.

The remaining 518 misses (32.9%) have no nearby companion truth —
these are pure sync or coverage failures.

**The RR73 result is the single biggest production bug surfaced
to date.** 503 RR73 truths in hard-200; only 2 recovered. 99.6% miss
rate, on top of which 282 of these missed RR73s were at strong SNR
(≥ -10 dB) — these are LOUD signals that pancetta simply doesn't
decode.

Other types have roughly comparable recall (~55-65%), suggesting the
non-RR73 misses follow the capture-effect / general-sensitivity
profile.

### CC — sync-vs-payload split

For each strong miss, check pancetta's emissions within ±5 Hz / ±0.3 s
of the truth position:
- **99.2% (1563/1576)** have NO nearby pancetta emission
- 0.8% (13/1576) have a nearby emission with different text

→ Strong-signal misses are essentially all sync/coverage failures, NOT
text-format mismatches. Pancetta isn't surfacing these positions as
candidates at all. (This rules out the hb-023-class "display bug
masquerading as sensitivity" pattern.)

## Diagnostic DD — RR73 deep-dive

Targeted audit: for each of the 503 RR73 truths, what does pancetta emit
near the truth position?

- 2 (0.4%) exact text match
- 0 (0.0%) nearby with different text
- **501 (99.6%) NO nearby pancetta emission**

Plus a corpus-wide count:
- pancetta emits ` RR73`-suffix messages: **5** (total across all 200 WAVs)
- jt9 truth has ` RR73`-suffix messages: 503

Pancetta produces ~100× fewer RR73 messages than jt9 expects across the
full corpus. This is a fundamental decoder gap, not a sync-window or
SNR issue — the strongest misses are at +21 dB.

## Code-inspection findings on the RR73 bug

The parser in `pancetta-ft8/src/message.rs::parse_type1_standard` (line
~1367) handles RR73 correctly: when `igrid4 = MAXGRID4 + 3 = 32403`,
`unpackgrid` returns `token = Some("RR73")` and the standard-type
branch sets `StandardMessageType::RR73`. Round-trip test for
`"K1DEF W1ABC RR73"` passes — clean encode/modulate/decode works.

So the bug is NOT in:
- The parser (RR73 path exists and works)
- `is_plausible` (RR73 falls into the Standard-with-valid-callsigns
  bucket that passes)
- The encoder (round-trip works)

The bug IS somewhere between:
- Spectrogram synthesis (sync-search range / sub-bin coverage)
- Costas-pattern matching
- LDPC convergence on RR73-shaped codewords
- The decoder hot path's candidate-selection heuristics

Given:
- Misses at +21 dB SNR (sync should be trivial)
- ALL misses are no-nearby-emission (no sync) not nearby-wrong-text
- Synthetic round-trip works

The most likely mechanism: pancetta's decoder pipeline at some step
**rejects** valid RR73 codewords before they reach the parser. Could be
a CRC verification path, a duplicate-suppression step, or a plausibility
shortcut that rules them out as "noise-like."

This deserves a focused next-batch debug session. NOT attempted as a
fix in this batch — the mechanism isn't obvious from code inspection
and a wrong fix could regress non-RR73 recall.

## Strategic / substance-check notes

- **The capture-effect at 67% is the largest single mechanism**
  blocking strong-signal recall. Any joint-decoding /
  coherent-cancellation work (hb-079 / hb-080 / hb-086 families) would
  directly address this. Those have been worked extensively without
  major production-recall ship; revisiting with the corpus-scale
  capture-locked count (1058) as the recall headroom may justify
  another attempt.
- **RR73 is the second-biggest mechanism** and looks like a fixable
  bug, not a fundamental limit. The 501-truth pool is small in
  absolute terms but a 99.6% miss rate on a common QSO-completion
  message is unusual. Worth a focused debug session.
- **CC (99.2% no-emission) confirms it's a SYNC/COVERAGE problem,
  not a parse/format problem.** Unlike hb-023 (text format bug), this
  isn't fixable at the display layer.
- **The cumulative recall headroom from coverage**: roughly 1576 TPs
  at ≥-10 dB alone (32.4% strong miss). Adding the weaker buckets
  brings it to ~30-35% total miss vs jt9 across the whole corpus.
  That's much bigger than any single FP-filter improvement.
