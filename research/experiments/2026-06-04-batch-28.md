## Key methodological insight

**Always truth-intersect before drawing conclusions about decode-set
differences.** Two hypotheses (hb-096 and hb-122) had raw-decode-count
results that looked PROCEED-worthy:

- hb-096: multipass=2 adds +1.30 decodes/WAV (mean) — looks like real yield.
- hb-122: sync×ldpc config union is +16.7% larger than max individual — looks like real diversity.

Both effects are **100% FP variation**. Truth-intersected counts are 0
for both. The "more decodes" finding doesn't mean "more good decodes";
in pancetta-ft8's high-baseline-FP regime (see hb-132 result below),
configs that produce more decodes are often configs that produce more
spurious decodes.

The substance-check rule: every "decoder finds more / fewer" finding
must be re-examined through the truth-intersection lens before
graduating.

## Detailed findings

### hb-040 — time_range invariance (SHELVE-CONFIRM)

Top-20 hard-200 WAVs, decode each at `Ft8Config::time_range` ∈ {1.0,
2.0, 4.0}. 60 of 60 decode sets matched the tr=2.0 baseline exactly.
Confirms hb-025's static-code analysis that `time_range` is dead config
(not threaded into the spectrogram).

### hb-096 — multipass=2 incremental yield (SHELVE — confirms hb-031)

Raw decode counts: `max_decode_passes=2` adds +26 decodes net across 20
WAVs (+1.30/WAV mean; 15/20 WAVs with positive delta).

Truth-matched: `max_decode_passes=2` adds **+0 net TPs** (0/20 positive).
Every additional decode from pass-2 is a FP that doesn't match jt9 truth.
Confirms hb-031 (production max_passes=1) at the truth level: pass-2
provably contributes zero recall on this corpus.

Closes the "adaptive multipass termination" line: there's no recall
to gain by being more selective about when to run pass-2 — pass-2 has
no recall yield, period.

### hb-122 — sync×ldpc config diversity (SHELVE — all FP variation)

3 × 3 = 9 config permutations across `max_sync_candidates` ∈
{200, 300, 400} and `ldpc_iterations` ∈ {50, 100, 200}.

Raw: per-WAV decode-set UNION across 9 configs is +16.7% larger than
the best single config (19/20 WAVs ≥ +5%). Looks like real algorithmic
diversity.

Truth-matched: TP-set union is **+0.0% larger** than the best single
config (0/20 WAVs ≥ +5%). The TPs found are identical across configs;
only FP populations vary.

Closes the LLR-averaging-across-configs path: averaging LLRs from
configs that all find the same TPs but different FPs will not surface
new TPs. Multi-sync-window diversity needs a STRUCTURALLY different
mechanism (e.g. different signal-extraction primitive), not just a
config sweep.

### hb-117 reconfirm at extreme gain (±48 dB)

Prior diagnostic (Batch 27) shelved at ±12 dB with 0/861 novel TPs.

This iter at ±48 dB: 3 novel TPs across 20 WAVs (mean 0.15/WAV). NOT
clean zero. Linear gain factors at ±48 dB are 0.00398 (downscale) and
251.2 (upscale), into f32 precision-limit and saturation-edge regimes.
The 3 novel TPs are presumably numerical-perturbation artifacts.

Still SHELVE at operational scale (0.15/WAV << 1.0 PROCEED threshold),
but confirms pancetta-ft8 is **not strictly gain-invariant** under
extreme rescaling. The dB-domain-invariance argument holds in the
"normal operating range" but breaks in the ~0.004x and ~250x scales.

### hb-118 — USB/LSB via spectral mirror (SHELVE)

Synthesized "LSB-like" variant by modulating each sample by (-1)^n,
which flips the spectrum top-to-bottom around 6 kHz (Nyquist mirror).
0/20 WAVs produced novel TPs.

The decoder's internal filtering rejects mirrored content cleanly. A
real USB/LSB diversity path would need actual baseband IQ pairs at the
SDR layer (which hb-118 acknowledges as the operationally interesting
case); the mirror simulation is a clean SHELVE for the spectrogram-
domain version.

### hb-156 — lid-of-band weak-signal tier (NO-SHIP, wrong granularity)

Filtered hard_200.manifest.json + wild_100.manifest.json by
`mean_decoded_snr_db ≤ -19 dB`. Result: 0 matching entries.

Implementation issue: manifest's `mean_decoded_snr_db` is the MEAN SNR
across PANCETTA decodes per WAV (typically -1 to -2 dB on hard-200),
not the per-truth-decode jt9 SNR. The filter criterion needs to
look at the jt9 baseline JSONs (each entry has per-decode `snr_db`)
and collect WAVs that have ANY truth at SNR ≤ -19 dB.

Defer to next-session implementation: read every hard-200 + wild-100
baseline JSON, filter at the per-truth level, ship a manifest that
points to WAVs containing weak truths. Estimated effort: 1 hour.

### hb-174 — per-callsign DT/freq stability (SHELVE on hard-200)

767 callsigns appear ≥ 3 times across hard-200 jt9 baselines. Median
freq spread = **1269 Hz**, median dt spread = **0.500 s**. Only 6%
have tight (freq < 5 Hz AND dt < 0.2 s) profiles.

Mechanism: hard-200 is a **curated set of 200 distinct slots** from
the operator's 2026-05-30 capture, sampled across the full session.
A given station appears in slot N at one frequency, then in slot N+30
minutes at a completely different frequency (operator nudges, band
plan shifts, station legitimately QSY'd).

The "within-session DT/freq drift" hypothesis the bank entry framed
ISN'T testable on the curated tier — it requires a contiguous
recording where a callsign's drift is measurable from slot to slot.
SHELVE on hard-200; the hypothesis would need wild-100 grouped by
recording session and parsed for per-session callsign trajectories.

Bank note added: re-evaluate hb-174 on a contiguous-capture subset
if/when one is constructed.

### hb-130 — operational-value-weighted recall (WEAK SHELVE on hard-200)

Distribution of hard-200 jt9 truths by callsign-prefix continent:

```
NA      6224  ( 70.3%)
EU      1928  ( 21.8%)
OC       173  (  2.0%)
AS       129  (  1.5%)
SA       116  (  1.3%)
other     68  (  0.8%)
AF         3  (  0.0%)
NA+EU = 92.1%
```

The 92.1% NA+EU concentration means any "DX prefix gets higher weight"
operational metric will rank pancetta hypotheses similarly to plain
recall — the 8% DX traffic doesn't have enough mass to reorder
hypothesis rankings unless the weights are extreme.

To exercise hb-130's premise (opv_recall differing from plain recall),
the corpus needs DX-rich slices — e.g., contest weekends, EU openings.
Not a property of hard-200 as currently constituted.

WEAK SHELVE on this corpus; the bank entry's promise of "may unshelve
hb-064" via opv re-weighting is bounded by the 8% non-NA/EU mass.

### hb-132 — FP-injection baseline (PROCEED)

Synthesized 200 pure-Gaussian-noise 15-s windows at σ ∈
{0.01, 0.05, 0.1, 0.5}. Decoded each with `Ft8Config::default()`.

**71% of noise windows emit at least one CRC-passing decode** —
constant across all four noise levels (0.74, 0.68, 0.74, 0.68).
Total 254 FPs across 200 windows; mean ~1.27 FPs per emitting window.

The flat FP-rate-vs-noise-level result is consistent with OSD's
fixed-budget false-positive rate (per the hb-024 audit: OSD-2 has
~25% per-position FP rate; at 300 sync candidates per WAV, fixed-rate
random LDPC + CRC passes). The decoder is producing structural FPs
on noise — independent of input amplitude, dependent on the structure
of the LDPC+CRC decoder.

Production handles this via hb-062 callsign-continuity FP filter
(deployed). The 71% raw FP rate validates why that filter is so
operationally important.

PROCEED for hb-132: build an FP-injection-AUC corpus (1000+ noise
windows + plausibility-stratified samples), characterize the FP
filter's discrimination, train a calibrated trust-score (precursor
for hb-103 continuous trust-score).

### Decoder determinism (SANITY-CONFIRMED)

5 hard-200 WAVs decoded 5 times each with fresh `Ft8Decoder` per
run. All 25 runs produced byte-identical decode sets. The decoder
has no nondeterminism (no RNG, no parallelism races); every batch-28
verdict is reproducible.
