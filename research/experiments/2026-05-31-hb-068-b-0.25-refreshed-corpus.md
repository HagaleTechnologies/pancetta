## Why re-check

hb-068 GRADUATED 2026-05-30 at `sync_time_interp_delta_scale = 0.3`.
During that variant scan, a parallel-agent run at b-0.25 on the
*old* hard-200 had reached rec=4623 / novel=914 vs the chosen 0.3's
rec=4621 / novel=914 — i.e. +2 hard-200 over the production choice
on that corpus. Synth had not been validated at 0.25, so we shipped
0.3 as the conservative default and queued a "finer-scale sweep
including synth" follow-up.

The 2026-05-31 corpus refresh replaced 100 of 200 hard-200 entries
with today's K5ARH 20m captures. That changes the empirical
optimum — the refreshed corpus could favor either 0.25 or 0.3, and
both old-corpus reference points are no longer comparable. Cheapest
question to answer: re-run b-0.25 on the *new* full 5-tier and
diff against the current main.json baseline (b-0.3 on refreshed
corpus).

## Method

Single 5-tier eval at `sync_time_interp_delta_scale = 0.25`,
compared head-to-head against `research/scorecards/main.json`
(b-0.3, refreshed corpus, same harness commit `0f8299ec`-equivalent
config). All other knobs at production defaults.

```
./target/release/eval --mode ft8 \
  --tier fixtures,synth-clean,synth-doppler,curated-hard-200,curated-hard-1000,wild-100 \
  --sync-time-interp-delta-scale 0.25 \
  --output research/scorecards/sweep/hb068-b-0.25-refreshed-5tier.json
```

## Interpretation

- **Composite is worse at b-0.25.** The hard-200 weight
  (`real_decode_rate_hard_200: 0.5`) dominates and the −6 rec there
  pulls the score down. Synth-clean@90% is preserved at −20 dB
  (the +2 dB hb-044 rescue holds at 0.25), so the synth axis is not
  a tiebreaker.
- **Old-corpus advantage did not transfer.** On the old hard-200,
  b-0.25 had been +2 rec over b-0.3 (4623 vs 4621). On the refreshed
  hard-200 it's −6 rec (4936 vs 4942). The +2 vs −6 swing tracks the
  100-WAV K5ARH 20m injection — those captures evidently favor the
  slightly stronger 0.3× delta, while the OLD upper half of hard-200
  (rare-DXCC chasing audio) marginally favored 0.25×.
- **hard-1000 and wild-100 move +4 each**, which is within the
  per-tier noise floor for a 5-tier run on this scale. Not enough
  signal on its own to argue for b-0.25.
- **Novels drop −136 on hard-200** — this is the standout
  asymmetry. The smaller delta lets fewer marginal-fit candidates
  pass downstream stages, so the novel pool shrinks much more than
  recall does. Useful diagnostic: confirms 0.25 is biased toward
  "decoder pickiness," 0.3 toward "decoder reach."

## Decision

**KEEP-b-0.3.** Composite regression + hard-200 regression at fixed
synth gain leaves no axis on which b-0.25 wins on the refreshed
corpus. No code change. The hb-068 graduation defaults stand.

Documented as the planned follow-up's negative result — closes the
"could we safely retune the default down" question with a clean no
under current corpus conditions.

## What changed about the answer (vs the old-corpus parallel-agent finding)

The OLD-corpus result that motivated this re-check was: on hard-200
only (single tier), b-0.25 saw rec=4623 / novel=914 vs b-0.3's
rec=4621 / novel=914 — a +2 / 0 delta, well within noise but
suggestive. The implicit hypothesis was "the optimum might be at
0.25 with synth still preserved." The refreshed-corpus answer is:
the optimum still includes 0.3 for hard-200, the +2 was noise, and
the OLD vs REFRESHED hard-200 swing is large enough (−6 rec) to
overwhelm any old-corpus signal. The OLD-corpus +2 was a
corpus-specific artifact, not a fundamental b-scale property.

## Implications for the hb-068 family

- **b-scale = 0.3 is the production setting** on the refreshed
  corpus too — confirmed, not just defaulted-into.
- **The finer-scale sweep is not worth running broader.** Variants
  ∈ {0.2, 0.35, 0.4} would each take ~25 min and the per-corpus
  swing seen here (Δ ~10 hard-200 between 0.25 and 0.3 on the same
  k-flag) is already at the noise limit. Further sweeps would
  produce results that don't generalize across corpus refreshes.
- **hb-069** (linear-power-domain interp) is a separate mechanism
  and still worth chasing under its own priority. This result does
  not bear on it.
