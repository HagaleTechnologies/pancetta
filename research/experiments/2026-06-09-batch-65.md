## Headline

The user redirected: "We have many gigabytes of real-world WAVs. Do we need to reconsider what we have? Do we have a good standard way of evaluating the corpora for comparison?" Batch 65 builds the framework + runs it across 14 corpora (8 curated + 6 raw recording-day samples). The findings:

1. **Pancetta has 25,106 WAV recordings (8.5 GB) spanning 41 days.** Curated corpora total ~2,177 entries — less than **9% of available data**.
2. **The autonomous loop's "10 confirmed shelvings" reframes**: 9 of 10 are valid (FDR, auto_passband, etc.), but **hb-237 cross-sequence A7's measurement was rigged by the truth-labeling pipeline** (chrono_replay truth comes from running pancetta-ft8 *without* cross_seq A7, so any cross-seq-recovered decode is "FP" by construction — not by mechanism failure).
3. **hb-244 soft combiner's "regime doesn't exist on synthetic" finding is contradicted by real data**: raw_20260530 has 324 max-repeats-by-one-callsign in 500 sampled slots. The mechanism's natural-fading regime IS in the raw recordings.
4. **FDR's shelving is robust**: across ALL 14 corpora, FreeText / Telemetry / DXpedition / Contest counts are **0**. The operator's bands don't carry these types regardless of which day we sample.

**Cumulative recovery (Batches 33-65)**: +369 TPs (unchanged — analysis batch, no mechanism ships).

## What shipped

`pancetta-research/examples/batch65_corpus_characterize.rs` — standardized corpus characterizer. Takes a manifest path OR a directory glob, decodes each WAV, computes mechanism-relevant metrics purely from decoder output (no truth labels required), outputs a markdown report + JSON sidecar.

Metrics per corpus:
- Volume — slot count, time span (when timestamps extractable from filenames)
- **Signal density** (auto_passband regime) — mean / p50 / p90 / p99 decodes-per-slot
- **Message-type distribution** (FDR regime) — per-type counts
- **SNR distribution** (LLR whitening / tier-presets) — 5dB-binned histogram
- **QSO-continuity index** (hb-237 regime) — fraction of within-30s adjacent slot pairs where a prior callsign reappears
- **Repeat-heavy index** (hb-244 regime) — distribution of callsign appearance counts; max_repeats; top-10 list
- **Drift index** (Batch 50 freq tracker regime) — median std-dev of decoded freq across per-callsign receptions

`scripts/batch65_characterize_all.sh` — runs the characterizer across all 8 curated manifests + 6 raw recording-day samples (500-slot subset per day). Total wall-clock ~90 min on this machine.

JSON sidecars at `research/corpus/characterizations/*.json` (14 files).

## What the table tells us

### 1. FDR is correctly shelved — the regime doesn't exist on this operator's bands

Standard message type = **99.0-100% across all 14 corpora**. FreeText / Telemetry / DXpedition / Contest = **0 in every corpus**. NonStdCall is the only "unusual" type at 0-1.7% of decodes. FDR's per-message-type confidence gate has nothing to gate.

### 2. hb-237 cross-sequence A7 was measured on a truth-rigged corpus

chrono_replay is a 300-slot subset of the 2026-05-30 recording session (1.2-hour span). The truth labels for chrono_replay come from running **pancetta-ft8 itself** on that audio. Anything cross_seq A7 produces that pancetta-ft8-without-cross_seq-A7 didn't produce is, by construction, labeled "FP" — not because the mechanism is wrong but because the truth set excludes it.

This means **Batch 54's "0 TPs from 796 cs_emits"** is a measurement artifact, not a mechanism verdict. To fairly measure hb-237 we need either:
- WSJT-X-derived truth labels (which include AP/a7 decodes)
- A larger curated corpus sampled across days, with truth from a "best-effort" pipeline that includes cross_seq A7

raw_20260530's **99.4% QSO continuity over 499 pairs** confirms the regime IS available. raw_20260424 (69.0% over 303 pairs) is another candidate. **The mechanism may have been wrongly shelved.**

### 3. hb-244 soft combiner: real-world data has the regime

raw_20260530 sample has max_repeats = 324 by one callsign in 500 sampled slots. The full day has 2066 slots, so total repeats by that callsign over the whole day could exceed 1300. raw_20260428 has max_repeats 262 in 500 slots. These are repeat-heavy in a way synthetic Gaussian noise cannot reproduce.

The Batch 62-63 finding (combiner inert on synthetic) was correct AS-MEASURED but doesn't generalize to real-world data. The natural-fading regime is in the raw recordings.

### 4. Auto-passband's "dense corpus harmful" finding holds — but at different intensities

p99 decodes/slot:
- hard_200: 100 (extreme dense slots)
- hard_1000: 41, lid_of_band: 41
- chrono_replay: 34
- raw_20260419: 4 (dead band — would be auto_passband's regime)
- raw_20260425/26: 4 (dead bands)

Dead-band slots would be auto_passband's natural regime. But the dead bands also have ~1 decode/slot total, so the recall opportunity is tiny.

### 5. The autonomous loop's "+0 TPs since Batch 53" framing was misleading

Of the 12 measurement-negative batches, only **6 measured on hard-200** (or a subset of it). The findings generalize across the **2,177 curated entries** but don't necessarily generalize to the **22,929 uncharacterized slots** in raw recordings. Two of those uncharacterized slots' regimes (raw_20260530 repeat-heavy, raw_20260424 QSO-continuous) might revive mechanisms we shelved.

## Recommended Batch 66

1. **Build `curated/ft8/repeat_heavy_530.manifest.json`** — sample slots from raw_20260530 where the same callsign appears 3+ times within 45 min. Run truth labeling on it.
2. **Re-measure hb-244 soft combiner** on the new corpus. The Batch 62 conclusion ("sync fails entirely below cliff on synthetic") doesn't predict the real-world outcome.
3. **Build `curated/ft8/qso_continuous_530.manifest.json`** — sample sequential slot pairs from raw_20260530 with QSO continuity. Run truth labeling using a "best-effort" pipeline that explicitly INCLUDES cross_seq A7 outputs.
4. **Re-measure hb-237** against the new corpus. The Batch 54 finding is suspect because of the truth-label rigging.
5. **Use the characterization framework as a corpus-quality gate**: future corpora should publish their characterization JSON alongside the manifest. Existing curated manifests should get characterizations published as sidecars.
