## Active bank shape post-ideation — TOP-5 BRIEFING

The five highest-priority *attackable* hypotheses after this pass, ranked by
priority then session-readiness:

1. **hb-093 — per-position residual SNR pre-decode gate (priority 0.52, scope-able 2 sessions)** —
   Tonight's V3/hb-088 shelves both confirmed sub-Costas residuals are
   noise-dominated for the interfering-neighbor regime. An *up-front*
   per-position residual SNR estimator (cheap energy-vs-local-noise probe
   on the post-hb-079 residual) could gate which residual positions are
   worth decoding at all. Attacks elapsed (skip noise positions early)
   without changing decode semantics, and unlocks a future
   "AP-with-residual-SNR-prior" mechanism that wasn't viable for hb-087.
   Cheap, measurable kill-switch (energy probe correlation with truth-pos
   decodability on top-20 hard-200).

2. **hb-048 a7 (priority 0.45, Session 2 in flight)** —
   Joe Taylor's template-cross-correlation against next-slot residual.
   Already through Session 1 design; Session 2 is template generator +
   primitive. Survives mr-007 (callsign tracker state is being added in
   pancetta-ft8 anyway; no cross-Costas mining). The structural picture
   reinforces this: a7 finds *future* signals constrained by *prior
   knowledge*, the only constraint shape that hasn't been refuted.

3. **hb-089 — multi-cycle coherent residual accumulation (priority 0.48)** —
   Q65 averages over multiple receive windows. hb-085 shelved
   cross-cycle-on-residual structurally ("no peer to average with"),
   but only at the **decode-position level**. The new shape:
   accumulate the *residual spectrogram itself* across the 5 same-slot
   sub-windows in a 90s recording AFTER multipass saturates, then run
   one final decode pass on the accumulated residual. Cross-cycle of
   *the cleaned signal*, not of the original. Different mechanism
   from hb-074/075 (which average the raw spectrogram pre-subtract).

4. **hb-064 Session 3 — DIA-OSD with larger training corpus (deferred 0.42)** —
   Session 2 SHELVED on -135 novels at +5.3× per-bit precision.
   Session 3 was scoped: bigger corpus (full hard-200/-1000 instead of
   25 WAV head). Out-of-distribution drift is the diagnosed regression
   source. Defensible — paper still claims -97% TEP at SNR=2dB.

5. **hb-091 — a8-style early-decode latency reduction (priority 0.42)** —
   WSJT-X-Improved 3.x ships "a8" that decodes the in-QSO station 0.5-1s
   earlier. For pancetta's autonomous QSO loop (Phase 5 active),
   early-decode is operational gold — completes more QSOs per pass when
   propagation is variable. Operational, not corpus-recall. Different
   target axis than tonight's offline-recall shelves.

The top-5 includes one diagnostic-attacker (hb-093), one external-source
plan-sized (a7), one residual-quality refinement (hb-089), one neural OSD
retrain at scale (hb-064), and one operational latency win (hb-091).
Diversified across the four open territories identified in this pass.

### hb-089 — Multi-cycle coherent residual accumulation
- Territory: A (residual quality)
- Prior: Q65 averages over receive windows; hb-085 SHELVED naive
  cross-cycle-on-residual due to "no peer at the decode position",
  but that's only true for post-subtract DECODE positions. A 90s
  multi-slot WAV contains 5 same-slot sub-windows; their RESIDUALS
  (post-hb-079 multipass saturation) all share the same noise floor
  position relative to the slot start. Accumulating the complex
  residual across the 5 sub-windows BEFORE the final decode pass is
  structurally different from accumulating the post-subtract decode
  position.
- mr-007: pancetta-ft8 has `coherent_multipass` infra (hb-079) and
  the complex spectrogram (hb-075). The 5-sub-window decomposition
  is the existing cross-cycle path (hb-056). NEW work: accumulating
  the post-multipass residual across sub-windows BEFORE the new-
  candidate decode pass. Plumbing exists.
- Kill-switch: top-20 hard-200 diagnostic — for missed truths whose
  callsign appears in 2+ same-slot sub-windows of the recording,
  measure per-position residual SNR before and after 5-window
  coherent accumulation. PROCEED if median SNR improvement ≥ 2 dB.
- Effort: 1-2 sessions (Session 1 = diagnostic, Session 2 =
  implement + sweep).
- NOT replicating hb-085: that attacked DECODE positions; this
  attacks RESIDUAL SPECTROGRAM bins. Cleared.

### hb-090 — Phase-coherent matched filter at truth coordinates
- Territory: A (residual quality)
- Prior: cited explicitly in hb-088 shelve journal as the
  "structurally different mechanism family" not tested. Phase-
  coherent matched filtering REJECTS energy at adjacent freq_bins
  via the matched-filter response shape, which is exactly the
  interferer-leakage discriminator that single-position spectrogram
  mining lacks.
- mr-007: pancetta-ft8 has complex spectrogram (hb-075) and the
  `extract_symbols_via_fft` shape. The matched filter would extract
  symbols via time-domain correlation with the Costas-aligned tone
  templates at the truth's exact (freq, dt), rejecting adjacent-bin
  energy via filter selectivity. Plumbing: NEW per-candidate IQ
  extractor + matched filter; reuses existing audio buffer.
- Kill-switch: top-20 hard-200 diagnostic comparing matched-filter
  LLR sign-agreement with truth codeword vs hb-088's spectrogram-
  max-log result. PROCEED if matched-filter median sign-agreement
  ≥ 70% (vs hb-088's 50.6%).
- Effort: 2-3 sessions (Session 1 = matched-filter primitive +
  diagnostic, Session 2-3 = production wiring + sweep).
- NOT replicating hb-088: that used max-log demod on the
  spectrogram; this uses matched-filter demod on the audio.
  Cleared.

### hb-092 — Codeword-based NMS dedup
- Territory: B (precision)
- Prior: hb-036 shelve journal explicitly cites this as "the brute-
  force answer" to the duplicate-vs-distinct problem: just decode
  both candidates and dedup by codeword. NMS family closed for
  pre-decode filtering, but post-decode codeword-dedup is
  unexplored.
- mr-007: pancetta decodes all candidates that pass sync (the
  rayon-parallel decode path). Same-codeword duplicates ARE present
  in the output stream and get deduped at the `unique_decoded`
  HashSet level — but ONLY by exact text match. Subtle issue:
  fractional-DT or fractional-freq duplicates of the same codeword
  may differ in displayed dt/freq and pass through. Post-decode
  codeword-binary dedup (compare the 174-bit codewords) catches
  these.
- Kill-switch: count fraction of `unique_decoded` outputs on top-20
  hard-200 that have a codeword-binary match with another output
  in the same WAV but differ in (freq, dt) by ≥ 5 Hz / 50 ms.
  PROCEED if ≥ 5% have such collisions (recall: zero risk;
  precision lever).
- Effort: 1 session.
- NOT replicating hb-036: hb-036 was PRE-decode score-based
  suppression. This is POST-decode codeword-binary dedup. Different
  point in pipeline; recall-preserving by construction. Cleared.

### hb-095 — Neural soft-demod replacement for max-log LLR
- Territory: D (ML augmentation, bounded scope)

  about neighbor patterns.
- mr-007: pancetta has the LLR computation primitive
  (`par_compute_soft_llrs_db`). NEW work: replace it with a small

  Risk: this changes the LDPC input distribution; an existing-LDPC
  retune may be required.
- Kill-switch: train tiny model on synth-clean; measure (a) per-bit
  sign-agreement on the truth codeword at SYNC-PASSING positions
  on hard-200 (control: max-log = 84% at sync-passing), (b)
  LDPC+CRC pass rate. PROCEED if (a) ≥ 88% and (b) doesn't drop.
- Effort: PLAN-SIZED (3-4 sessions).
- NOT replicating hb-088: hb-088 tested OSD's flip-enumeration
  capacity on max-log LLRs at sub-Costas positions. This replaces
  the LLR EXTRACTOR itself with a learned one; orthogonal to where

- WILD-CARD: yes. Bank as wild_card true.

### hb-096 — Adaptive multipass termination by decode-rate
- Territory: B (throughput; latency cousin of hb-091)
- Prior: hb-080 graduated N=3 multipass with `coherent_multipass_iterations`

  pays the wall-clock cost on EVERY WAV; on WAVs where pass 1+2
  saturate, passes 3+ are pure overhead. An adaptive termination
  based on the DECODE-COUNT delta (not residual-energy delta)

  kill-switch shape than hb-016.
- mr-007: hb-080's multipass loop has `pass_unique.is_empty() {
  break }` (the existing zero-decode short-circuit) but no threshold

  multipass_decode_floor { break }` after the existing zero check.
  Trivial plumbing.
- Kill-switch: sweep `multipass_decode_floor ∈ {1, 2, 3}` on
  hard-200; PROCEED only if elapsed -5% with recall preserved.
- Effort: 1 session.
- NOT replicating hb-016: hb-016 used residual ENERGY as the
  termination signal; this uses DECODE COUNT. Different metric,
  different lever. hb-016's failure was that the energy probe paid

### hb-097 — Subtract amplitude calibration via residual-energy minimization
- Territory: A (residual quality)
- Prior: hb-081 SHELVED (under-subtracting blocks multipass).
  hb-079 subtracts at full ML projection. But the ML projection
  assumes the rotor estimate is exact; rotor noise systematically
  biases the projection magnitude. A per-decode amplitude calibration
  step that finds the scale α ∈ [0.8, 1.2] minimizing local residual
  energy around the subtracted position could improve subtraction
  precision WITHOUT under-subtracting (the optimum is data-driven).
- mr-007: hb-079's `subtract_decode_coherent` applies a fixed full
  ML projection. Plumbing: add an α optimization step (1D line
  search over residual energy in a ±3 freq_bin × ±2 time_step
  window). ~10 evaluations per decode at minimal cost.
- Kill-switch: top-20 hard-200 diagnostic — measure per-decode α
  optimum distribution; PROCEED if median(|α - 1|) ≥ 0.05 AND
  follow-up multipass finds ≥ 1 additional decode per WAV with
  α-optimized subtract vs full ML.
- Effort: 1-2 sessions.
- NOT replicating hb-081: hb-081 used FIXED rotor-confidence
  thresholds (5, 10, 20, 40) and discrete scales; hb-097 uses
  data-driven α optimization per-decode. The shelve found "under-
  subtracting blocks multipass"; α-optimization can converge
  ABOVE 1.0 (over-subtract) when the rotor under-estimates the
  signal, which the fixed-threshold approach can't. Cleared.
