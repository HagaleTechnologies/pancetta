## Key findings from wsjtr comparison

### Where wsjtr is AHEAD of pancetta

- **Costas sync**: 3-position correlation + sync_bc/sync_abc partials
- **Sync normalization**: 40th-percentile across freq bins (adaptive)
- **Refinement**: 5×5 grid (dt, freq) via time-domain Goertzel
- **Downsampler**: cached 192k-point FFT + per-candidate band extract +
  3200-point IFFT (200 Hz complex baseband)
- **BP precision**: f64 tanh domain (vs pancetta f32 LLR)
- **OSD init**: zsum snapshots at iter 1 & 2 (vs pancetta's LLR-based)

### Where pancetta is AHEAD of wsjtr

- **6 layers of FP filtering** (hb-052/058/103/217 + degenerate_grid +
  digit_run) — wsjtr has effectively none
- **Neural OSD** with 600× OSD trial reduction at 80KB weights
- **Bootstrap-CI graduation policy** gating against shipping noise
- **Research apparatus**: hard-200/1000, lid_of_band, hypothesis bank
- **Autonomous station infrastructure**: QSO state machine, priority
  scoring, hamlib, multi-stream TX, cqdx.io integration

### Different mechanisms (not better/worse)

- **Cross-sequence A7**: wsjtr has cross-window; pancetta's hb-048 was
  within-window (and SHELVED). hb-237 implements cross-window.
- **AP modes 3-6**: wsjtr explicitly skips ("requires QSO state
  tracking"). Pancetta HAS QSO state — natural extension.
- **OSD design**: pancetta "first CRC-pass wins" (no dmin); wsjtr/WSJT-X
  "best by dmin". Different tradeoffs.

## Strategic posture (one sentence)

**Two cooperating Rust FT8 projects with non-overlapping scopes,
cross-citing each other's docs, sharing algorithmic findings, never
merging code, never depending on each other.**

This is the right shape for a small-community open-source ecosystem
post-WSJT-X stagnation.
