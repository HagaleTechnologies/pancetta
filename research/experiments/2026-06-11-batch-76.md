## Verdict

1. **Hard gate: SHELVE at every K.** The plan's K≥3 setting would discard
   three-quarters of true decodes. Even K≥1 costs 24% of TPs.
2. **Feature: real.** The ~30-point conditional-precision spread at K≥1 with
   75% TP coverage is hb-103 v2 feature material (alongside Diagnostic V's
   `decode_time_into_window`). Logged as **hb-245**, priority 0.35.
3. **Root cause of the coverage ceiling**: band-population churn plus corpus
   sparsity — only 3 of 12 days are dense (5/30, 5/31, 4/20 at 14–19 truth
   decodes/slot); 4/24–4/26 (66% of slots) are near-dead band. A trust DB
   accumulated from this corpus simply hasn't *seen* most of a dense day's
   active stations.
4. **Headroom caveat**: measured against the old osd=Some(2) FP pool. The
   shipped Batch 72 default already removed ~2.4× of those FPs, so the
   absolute FP pool this feature could attack in production is much smaller.
5. **Side-signal**: messages with no parseable callsign are 14× more FP-prone
   (1.4% of FPs vs 0.1% of TPs) — consistent with hb-062's coverage.
