## Motivation

mr-006's corpus survey
(`research/experiments/2026-05-25-corpus-expansion-survey.md`) graded
five potential real-world FT8 audio classes and concluded **exactly one**
is worth acquiring for pancetta's offline single-call eval architecture:
polar/auroral and TEP **real Doppler** captured via KiwiSDR. The other
four classes are either architecturally dead (DXpedition SuperFox,
splatter), marginal (contest QSB), or rarest to obtain (HF-mobile
flutter, subsumed by Doppler).

hb-073 is the operator-physical work that turns the mr-006 conclusion
into a usable eval tier. Two downstream hypotheses are blocked on this:

- **hb-015** (Doppler-resilient sync search via phase-coherent
  integration, priority 0.38) — the bank explicitly notes
  "best validated against a REAL Doppler tier (hb-073); the crude
  synth-doppler model may understate the gain (lacks true spread)."
- **hb-077** (phase-coherent SDR-IQ eval corpus, priority 0.20) — sibling
  enabler; KiwiSDR captures partially cover the same regime.

Without a real-Doppler tier, hb-015's measured gain on `synth-doppler`
is potentially misleading (the synth model is multiplicative cosine, not
true ionospheric spread). With it, hb-015 has a real denominator and the
priority bumps to ~0.42 per mr-006.

## Capture procedure summary

Full procedure: `docs/operations/2026-05-31-hb-073-kiwisdr-capture-procedure.md`.

Headline:

1. **Conditions to capture:** auroral (Kp ≥ 5, evening, 10/15 m, paths
   crossing the auroral zone), TEP (10/15 m, equatorial midpoint paths
   3-6 Mm, evening), greyline (terminator window, lower bands). K5ARH
   does not need to transmit.
2. **Site selection:** point a **remote** KiwiSDR (from
   http://kiwisdr.com/public/) at the **DX end** of the propagation
   path, not at K5ARH's end — the goal is to capture what the channel
   did to the propagating signal. Candidates by class: Norway / Finland /
   Iceland / Alaska for auroral; Caribbean / Brazil / Hawaii for TEP.
3. **kiwirecorder.py invocation:** canonical command with `--resample
   12000`, `-m usb`, `--lp 200 --hp 3000`, `--tlimit 1800`, plus a
   slot-aligned `python3 -c` waiter that sleeps until t-3s before the
   next FT8 boundary (:12 / :27 / :42 / :57). Output is 12 kHz mono i16
   WAV — pancetta-eval's required format.
4. **Ingestion:** WAVs land in `~/.pancetta/recordings/`, jt9 baseline
   per WAV (cached under `research/baselines/ft8/<sha>.json`), pancetta
   eval to find Doppler-rich WAVs (jt9 ≥ 5 decodes AND pancetta recovery
   ≤ 0.70), then curate `wild_doppler_50.manifest.json`.

Per-opening yield: 4-8 WAVs. Target across 5-8 openings: 30-60 WAVs
total, curated down to the 50 most Doppler-rich.
