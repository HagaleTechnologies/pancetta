# Batch 54 — hb-237 trust-gated cross-sequence A7 on chrono_replay full

**Date**: 2026-06-09
**Branch**: `iter/2026-06-09-batch-54`
**Status**: MEASUREMENT BATCH — definitive default-OFF call for hb-237 on RX-log corpora

## Headline

Production-shape probe of hb-237 cross-sequence A7 on chrono_replay full (300 sequenced slots) with the `CallsignContinuityFilter` trust gate engaged. **The trust gate works as designed** (filters 36% of offered seeds) **but doesn't prevent FP amplification** — every one of the 796 cross-sequence emissions is an FP. **0 of 796 are TPs.** Definitive default-OFF on RX-log corpora.

The root cause is corpus mismatch, not implementation: chrono_replay is a "what was on the band" capture, not a "my QSO partner replies" capture. Cross-sequence A7 requires active QSO continuity with pancetta's operator, which this corpus doesn't have.

**Cumulative recovery (Batches 33-54)**: +369 TPs (unchanged — measurement batch, no ships).

The trust gate works as designed — it rejects more than a third of offered seeds. But the seeds that pass aren't generating valid cross-sequence recoveries; they're generating structurally-plausible FPs at scale.

## Root cause: corpus mismatch (not implementation defect)

Cross-sequence A7 is designed for the scenario: **operator is in an active QSO, partner replies in next slot using same callsign at similar audio frequency**. The decoder uses prior-slot callsigns as a7-template seeds for the current slot.

`chrono_replay` is a "what was on the band during a 75-minute receive session" capture. The operator (pancetta) was not transmitting; nobody was REPLYING to pancetta. The callsigns decoded in slot N belong to OTHER QSOs between OTHER stations — those QSOs continue in slot N+1 but at different frequencies, with different callsigns, on different bands of the SSB rig.

So the seeds offered to slot N+1 are real callsigns the prior slot really decoded, but the slot N+1 audio has no signal MATCHING those seeds. The a7-template enumeration nevertheless tries to fit each seed against the audio noise, occasionally producing a structurally valid 77-bit payload that satisfies LDPC + CRC by chance — these are the 796 FPs.

The mechanism is **algorithmically correct** but **the trigger condition (active QSO partner expected) never fires** on RX-only corpora.

**Effectively identical per-slot FP rate** — the trust gate didn't change the per-slot FP density; it only reduced the SEED COUNT input by 36%. The remaining 64% of seeds still produce the same per-slot FP density. This suggests the mechanism is FP-producing per-slot regardless of how many seeds are offered, because the a7 template enumeration eventually finds noise-fitting payloads.

## Strategic implications

1. **hb-237 cross-sequence A7 is corpus-dependent in a way distinct from other Batch 50/52 mechanisms.** Auto-passband and the other "faithful but inapplicable" mechanisms can be re-measured on a different signal-density corpus. hb-237 requires a corpus type that **does not currently exist** in pancetta's test bank: a chronological recording where pancetta's operator is in active QSO and partner replies are present.

2. **The trust gate is doing its job, just not the job that helps here.** The 36% rejection rate is genuine FP-prevention at the seed boundary — without the gate, we'd see ~1248 cs_emits (extrapolating). With the gate, we see 796. The gate prevented ~452 FPs from being emitted, but the residual is still 100% FP because the mechanism's trigger condition doesn't fire on this corpus.

3. **Definitive default-OFF call.** No corpus pancetta currently has will show a positive measurement for hb-237. Until/unless an actual on-air QSO corpus is captured, the mechanism stays default-OFF. The cross-sequence cache + consumer + coordinator invocation are all wired up and verified; they cost zero when the flag is OFF.

4. **This closes hb-237's bootstrap-CI loop on the autonomous research path.** Future graduation requires hardware-in-the-loop testing during real QSOs (Phase 5 work). That's a meatspace-pending item, not an autonomous-loop item.

5. **The "faithful but inapplicable" family grows to 7.** Auto-passband, hb-242, wide-lag, three-stage cascade, npre2, four-th-pass-after-a7, and now hb-237 trust-gated. All shipped opt-in default-OFF. The pattern: peer-decoder mechanisms that pancetta's combination of (a) its own existing stack, (b) its corpus characteristics, and (c) its measurement gates filter out before graduation. **50%+ inert-on-measurement rate** for implementer-thread mechanisms continues to hold.
