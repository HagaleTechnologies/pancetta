**Conclusions**:

1. **Three-stage sync cascade is net-negative on hard-200** (-162 TPs at +1.0% precision). Same family as Batch 49's hb-242 / wide-lag findings — a mechanism that helps peer decoders doesn't help pancetta's full stack. Hypothesis: pancetta's existing parabolic_peak_refinement (hb-044) already provides Stage 2 with sub-symbol precision; ft8mon's third-stage post-decode refinement was designed to compensate for ft8mon's coarser Stage 2. When pancetta inherits the third stage on top of its already-precise Stage 2, the metric's local maxima drift the candidate away from truth optimum and into nearby noise lobes. **Default stays OFF. Shelve mechanism on hard-200; preserve for SIC-residual-quality-bottlenecked corpus**.

2. **Three of four mechanisms are inert on hard-200** — not regressions, just not exercised:
   - hb-237 Session 2 needs Session 3 (coordinator invocation) to fire
   - a8 needs live QSO state, which hard-200 doesn't have
   - npre2 needs OSD depth ≥3, pancetta defaults to depth 1

   These are all "structural OFF": flipping the flag accomplishes nothing without related state. Future work:
   - hb-237 Session 3: ~50-100 LOC in coordinator/ft8.rs to wire the consumer
   - a8: live-operation measurement (requires Phase 5 corpus)
   - npre2: re-measure with `Ft8Config::osd_depth = Some(3)` baseline

## Strategic implications

1. **Hard-200 alone can't validate every mechanism.** Three of four Batch 51 mechanisms are gated by state hard-200 doesn't carry (Session 3 wiring, QSO state, OSD depth). The corpus is good at validating decoder-internal mechanisms; live-operation mechanisms need a different corpus / live trial.

2. **The "knob doesn't engage in pancetta's stack" pattern continues** — Batches 41, 42, 43, 49, 51 all surfaced peer-decoder mechanisms that on hard-200 are net-zero or net-negative because pancetta has overlapping discipline (FP filters, sub-bin Costas, parabolic refinement) that absorbs or competes with the imported mechanism.

3. **Bootstrap-CI gate did its job again.** -162 TPs would have been a noticeable regression in production; the measurement caught it before any default flip.
