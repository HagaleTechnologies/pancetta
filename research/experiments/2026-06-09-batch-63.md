# Batch 63 — hb-244 cache-key tolerance widening + definitive shelve

**Date**: 2026-06-09 (rolling overnight into 2026-06-10)
**Branch**: `iter/2026-06-09-batch-63`
**Status**: IMPLEMENTATION + MEASUREMENT BATCH — added `soft_combiner_key_tolerance` knob (default 0 = byte-identical); widening provides no lift on the Batch 62 synthetic; hb-244 confirmed inapplicable on synthetic noise

## Headline

Batch 62 left hb-244 with an open question: was the inertness caused by cache-key drift (combiner's `(freq_bin, time_bin)` key shifts across noise-jittered receptions, preventing accumulation) or by a deeper mechanism issue? Batch 63 answers by adding a `soft_combiner_key_tolerance: u32` config knob and re-measuring with tolerance = 0 / 1 / 2 against the same Batch-62 synthetic.

**Result**: widening provides **zero lift at any tolerance**. All four configurations (standalone, tol=0, tol=1, tol=2) recover the same 3 of 6 receptions — those above the recovery cliff (-19 dB). The cliff is hard at -20 dB and none of the cache-key configurations bridges it.

**Conclusion**: the cache-key drift hypothesis was wrong. The actual issue is that **sync fails entirely below the cliff** on synthetic Gaussian noise; without sync candidates the combiner has no LLR input to accumulate. hb-244 is **definitively shelved on synthetic-noise corpora**; real-world data with natural multipath/fading/rapid-QSB might still exercise the mechanism's regime (sync detects + LDPC fails + accumulation succeeds) but synthetic Gaussian noise doesn't.

**Cumulative recovery (Batches 33-63)**: +369 TPs (unchanged).

## What shipped

`Ft8Config::soft_combiner_key_tolerance: u32` (default 0) — a new operator-facing knob. When > 0, the soft combiner's `combine()` consults neighbor buckets within ±tolerance in both `freq_bin` and `time_bin` dimensions, finding the best Hamming match across all candidate buckets.

Default-0 is byte-identical to the pre-Batch-63 behavior. The knob is preserved despite shipping default-OFF because:
1. The implementation is straightforward (~20 LOC + tests).
2. Operators in specific deployment environments (e.g., narrow-band repeaters where freq drift is structurally constrained) might benefit.
3. The Batch 64+ investigation may revisit it with a different corpus.

## Why the cliff is hard

At -19 dB (just above the cliff), the FT8 sync stage produces a candidate that passes through LDPC successfully (single reception). At -20 dB (just below), the sync stage either:
- Fails to find a Costas-correlated candidate at the right (freq, time), OR
- Finds a wrong-`(freq, time)` candidate that LDPC rejects.

Either way, the combiner has no input to accumulate from. The cliff is at the sync detection threshold, not the LDPC convergence threshold. Soft combiners help when sync detects + LDPC fails; they don't help when sync itself fails.

For the combiner's regime to exist on synthetic Gaussian noise, the synthetic signal would need to be JUST above the sync detection threshold (where sync occasionally detects) but JUST below the LDPC convergence threshold (where the codeword sometimes doesn't pass). That's a narrow SNR window. Synthetic noise is too unstructured to land there reliably.

**Real-world data is different**: natural fading + multipath produces SNR-modulated receptions where sync detects at moments of higher instantaneous SNR + LDPC fails at moments of lower SNR within the same 15s window — the sliding average across receptions IS in the combiner's regime. Synthetic single-σ Gaussian noise doesn't reproduce this structure.

## Strategic implications

1. **hb-244 is now confirmed shelved on synthetic corpora.** The Batch 62 finding (combiner inert on noise-jittered repeats) is not a cache-key drift artifact — it's structural. The combiner needs a corpus where sync occasionally detects below the LDPC convergence threshold; synthetic Gaussian noise doesn't provide this.

2. **Real-world corpus is now a HARD requirement for further hb-244 investigation.** This crystallizes the "corpus-diversity binding constraint" identified in Batch 61. We can build synth corpora for many regimes but the sync-detect-below-LDPC-converge regime cannot be cleanly synthesized.

3. **The "faithful but inapplicable" family stays at 10 (Batch 62 already counted hb-244).** Batch 63 doesn't add a new shelving; it confirms an existing one with a follow-up experiment.

4. **The `soft_combiner_key_tolerance` knob has standalone value.** Even without hb-244 graduating, the knob (default 0 / opt-in higher values) is useful for narrow-band deployment scenarios where freq drift is constrained. The implementation cost is amortized regardless of hb-244's status.
