## What we learned

### 1. Default-ON is a load-bearing decision

The Implementer Agent's choice to make hb-242 default-ON was based on
the spec's "targets slot-edge negative-dt bucket" framing. Reasonable
inference. But the corpus distribution matters: most hard-200 truths
are NOT slot-edge, so the mechanism's neutral-on-most + slight-FP-cost
gave -18 net.

**Lesson**: any new "natural max" mechanism that surfaces additional
candidates needs to either (a) ship default-OFF until per-corpus
measurement validates, or (b) ship paired with budget expansion
(`max_sync_candidates` bump) to absorb the new candidates.

### 2. Wide-lag baseline is closer to neutral

The wide-lag baseline (red2) mechanism shipped default-OFF and tested
at -4 TPs when enabled. That's much closer to neutral. The
percentile-normalization probably mitigates the FP-noise issue. With
tuning it could be positive.

**Next-batch candidate**: wide-lag baseline with `max_sync_candidates`
sweep to find the operating point.

### 3. Subsample DT refinement already shipped

Implementer B recognized that pancetta's existing hb-044
`parabolic_peak_refinement` already implements the textbook formula
the WSJT-X Improved spec describes. Updated docstring to cite both
hb-044 and the WSJT-X Improved spec; avoided duplicate implementation.

**Good clean-room hygiene**: cross-reference existing pancetta work
before assuming a mechanism is novel.

## Mechanism-by-mechanism detail

### hb-229 QSO partner band-collapse (SHIPPED, 0f1296f)

**Files**: `pancetta/src/coordinator/qso_filter.rs` (NEW, 236 LOC),
`pancetta/src/coordinator/mod.rs` (+1), `pancetta/src/coordinator/ft8.rs` (+42)

**Mechanism**: When `QsoManager` reports an active QSO, the observer
computes the partner's audio frequency, converts to `±60 Hz` window
in 6.25 Hz/bin units (matches existing hb-091 scoped fast-path
convention), passes through `freq_bin_scope` parameter to
`decode_window_with_ap_scoped`.

**Tests**: 13 new, all passing. Covers `partner_freq_to_bin_range`
correctness (centering, clamping, NaN safety) + observer behavior
(QSO active/inactive, override, lifecycle).

**Operational impact**: pure CPU win during in-flight QSO. Same
recall in the target band. Production impact is operational, not
hard-200 measurable (corpus has no live QSO state).

### hb-242 sync_bc partial Costas (SHIPPED OPT-IN, 49ddd63 + 174c5ed)

### Wide-lag baseline / red2 (SHIPPED OPT-IN, 49ddd63)
