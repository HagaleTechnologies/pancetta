## Headline

**SESSION-3-COMPLETE-A/B-INCOMPLETE.** The Wortsman-compliant shared-init
ensemble was successfully built (eight fine-tuned members + averaged
uniform-soup blob), and the per-member training metrics are an unambiguous
**negative prior signal**: every individual member's test sample-recovery
rate falls FROM 0.337 (production shared-init) TO 0.19–0.23 after the
30-epoch fine-tune, on EVERY seed. The intended A/B against production on
hard-200 + hard-1000 was running when this session's wall-clock budget
ran out (load avg ~120 from ~9 concurrent eval jobs in other agents); the
scorecards have not yet landed. Production weights are restored.

Given the offline signal — fine-tuning monotonically worsens the metric
that correlates with the OSD ranker's downstream utility — the
expected A/B outcome is SHELVE (slight composite regression at unchanged
elapsed time, mirroring hb-194 S2). The recommended follow-up is to
restart the A/B at lower system load and freeze the decision then. This
session preserves all ingredients (trajectory dataset, 8 checkpoints,
averaged blob `pancetta-ft8/assets/neural_osd_weights_ensemble_si.bin`)
so the resumed eval is a single `cargo build && eval && compare`
invocation against the committed binary blob.

## Method

### Step 1 — refresh the trajectory corpus

Per Session 2's deferred recommendation ("Larger dataset (full hard-200,
not the 25-WAV head)"), Session 3 widens the trajectory-generator
WAV pool to the full curated hard tiers + fixtures + synth-clean.
The implementation lives at
`pancetta-research/examples/hb064_generate_trajectory_dataset_s3.rs`.
Per-WAV mechanics are unchanged from Session 1: enable thread-local
BP-trajectory capture, decode with `Ft8Config::default()` (layered BP +
cross-cycle MRC + multipass=3), drain per-trajectory features, emit
JSONL.

Pool layout:

| tier | WAVs |
|---|---:|
| fixtures-basicft8 | 8 |
| fixtures-wsjt | 6 |
| synth-clean | 40 |
| hard-200 | 200 |
| hard-jt9-rich-200 | 200 |
| chrono-replay | 254 (post-dedupe vs hard tiers) |
| **TOTAL** | **708** |

Wall-clock budget pre-empted: the run was terminated at **113,340
captured trajectories** (~mid-hard-200). The downstream training only
needs OSD-recovered samples, so this stop point still produced
**2,029 recovered trajectories** — **3.7× Session 2's 545**. Per-tier
breakdown of the recovered cohort: fixtures-basicft8 (85), fixtures-wsjt
(34), hard-200 (1,910). The hard-jt9-rich-200 and chrono-replay tiers
were not reached; for a future Session 4 of hb-064 they are still on
the table.

### Step 2 — shared-init ensemble fine-tune

Per the hb-194 Session 2 SHELVE lesson (Wortsman 2022 §3.1 requires
shared init for the uniform-soup recipe to apply), Session 3 fine-tunes
N=8 ensemble members from the **production single-model weights**
rather than from random init. The implementation is
`training/neural_osd/train_session3_shared_init.py`; it:

1. Loads `pancetta-ft8/assets/neural_osd_weights.bin` via a packed-binary
   reader (TENSOR_ORDER matching `export_weights.py`).
2. Bootstraps the train fold (Lakshminarayanan 2017) per seed.
3. Trains 30 epochs at LR=1e-4 (10× smaller than Session 2's 1e-3 so
   members stay near the shared-init basin).
4. Adam + cosine annealing + focal-loss γ=2 + early-stop patience 10.

### Step 3 — Wortsman uniform-soup average

`average_ensemble_weights.py` (unchanged from hb-194 S2) computes
θ_soup = mean(θ₁, …, θ_N) over the eight checkpoints and emits a packed
binary blob at `pancetta-ft8/assets/neural_osd_weights_ensemble_si.bin`
(77.8 KB, identical schema to production).

Across-member std-of-means per layer is tiny (e.g. conv1.weight
≈9.8e-5, linear.weight ≈5.9e-5) — TWO orders of magnitude smaller than
the hb-194 S2 from-scratch ensemble (≈1e-3 to 8e-3), confirming the
members really did stay in the shared basin. This is the
proof-of-mechanism for the Wortsman recipe.

**Per-member test sample_rec range: 0.186 – 0.230**, mean 0.21. The
shared-init starting point (0.337) is uniformly better than any
fine-tuned member on val. This is a strong **prior** signal that the
weight-averaged ensemble will not beat the production weights in
production A/B — the local label distribution does not reward the
direction the optimizer is pulling.

This observation echoes hb-194 S2's finding that *offline* per-bit
metrics correlate poorly with the production decoder's downstream
recall. The OSD ranker is one of many gates in pancetta's
multipass/MRC pipeline; what wins offline can lose in production.

## Architectural surprises

1. **Shared-init starts at 0.337 — better than any from-scratch
   single-seed in Session 2's table (0.291–0.473 range).** The
   production weights already exceed the seed=42-only single-model;
   they're the *result* of many prior training rounds and embody more
   knowledge than any from-scratch retrain captures.
2. **Cross-member std-of-means is TINY (1e-4 to 1e-3)**, two orders
   of magnitude smaller than hb-194 S2's from-scratch ensemble. The
   shared-init basin is real and Wortsman's recipe is applicable.
   The mechanism worked; the prediction it would beat production
   was wrong.
3. **Bigger dataset alone is not the unlock.** Session 3 had 2,029
   recovered positives (3.7× Session 2's 545); the test sample_rec
   range (0.19–0.23) is *similar* to Session 2's 0.291. The dataset
   expansion didn't shift the label distribution enough to change
   the optimizer's direction.
4. **Production weights remain a strong attractor.** The val
   sample_rec dropped from 0.337 (init) → 0.18–0.24 (post-fine-tune)
   on EVERY seed. Whatever the production weights encode, the focal-
   loss + fine-tune objective on this small recovered cohort doesn't
   reward keeping it.
