## Hypothesis under test

> Form A from Session 1's recommendation: ship the ensemble-mean as the
> production OSD ranker. Replace `neural_osd_weights.bin` with N=8 packed
> blobs and run all 8 forward passes per OSD call. The Session 1
> recommendation was to start with output-space averaging despite the
> 8× inference cost.

This session's brief INSTEAD specified weight-space averaging (Wortsman
et al. 2022, "Model soups: averaging weights of multiple fine-tuned
models improves accuracy without increasing inference time", ICML) as
a drop-in replacement that preserves 1× inference cost. Wortsman's
"uniform soup" recipe is θ_soup = mean(θ_1, …, θ_N).

> **Caveat from Wortsman et al. 2022 §3.1:** model-soup works when the
> members all fine-tune from the same initialization basin. The paper's
> headline results are on ImageNet-fine-tuned CLIP, where every member
> starts from the same CLIP-zero-shot checkpoint and explores a single
> basin under different hyperparameters. **From-scratch training with
> different random seeds is NOT in scope of the Wortsman result** —
> members may land in different loss-landscape modes, in which case
> averaging produces a model on the chord between modes (typically
> worse than either endpoint).

The N=8 ensemble in Session 1 was trained from scratch with seed-only
diversity (no shared init). The Session 2 A/B is therefore also a test
of whether the loss-basin assumption holds for this particular CNN.
The result is consistent with "the basin assumption fails":
ensemble-mean performance is statistically indistinguishable from the
seed=42 single-model on hard-200 — i.e. the weight average preserves
seed=42's behavior approximately and does NOT capture the +55 % output-
space ensemble lift.

### Step 2: weight-space averaging

Implemented `training/neural_osd/average_ensemble_weights.py`. Loads
all 8 `.pt` files, computes element-wise mean of each named parameter
(`conv1.weight`, `conv1.bias`, `conv2.weight`, `conv2.bias`,
`conv3.weight`, `conv3.bias`, `linear.weight`, `linear.bias`), flattens
in the same TENSOR_ORDER as `export_weights.py`, and writes a 79,704-
byte packed-binary blob compatible with the Rust loader at
`pancetta-ft8/src/neural_osd_weights.rs`. Sentinel checksum
`conv1.weight[0] = −1.0903e-2` recorded in the script's stdout.

Across-member std of per-layer means is small (e.g. conv1.weight
~1.1e-3, conv2.weight ~1.6e-3); the linear layer has larger member
variation (~8.0e-3) — consistent with the N=8 members converging to
broadly similar early-conv filters but slightly different output
heads.

### Elapsed-time anomaly

The +82 % elapsed-time regression at unchanged inference architecture
is surprising and worth flagging for any follow-up. Plausible
explanations (none verified — out of scope for the SHELVE decision):

1. **OSD enumeration depth doubled.** The averaged-weights linear-layer
   head may produce a flatter posterior than any single seed, leading
   the OSD selection to enumerate more flip patterns before
   converging.
2. **Different code paths exercised.** A weight blob that ranks bits
   differently can push BP failures into OSD that previously
   short-circuited (and vice versa), shifting average-case decode
   cost.
3. **Curated tier TTFD growth.** Per the stdout, curated tier TTFD
   moved from p50=0.015 s to p50=0.055 s on hard-200 — a 3.7× per-WAV
   median decode time. Whatever the mechanism, the averaged weights
   are spending more time on the hard tail.

Whatever the root cause, the elapsed regression alone — independent of
the marginal-negative recall — disqualifies this blob for production.

### Per-tier CI conclusions

All four primary delta CIs straddle 0 with 95 % confidence. The brief
required hard-200 recall CI to be CI-significant AND positive for
GRADUATE; the actual interval is [−15, +1] — symmetric around the
negative point estimate, with the upper bound brushing 0 from below.
There is no statistical evidence that the averaged-weights blob is
better than the single-model baseline; there is weak (non-significant)
evidence the point estimate is negative.

### Why Session 1's +55 % did not translate

Two non-exclusive hypotheses:

1. **Output-space averaging is qualitatively different from weight-
   space averaging when members occupy different loss basins.** The
   N=8 ensemble's Session 1 win was an output-space mean of N=8 forward
   passes (run all 8 models, average their per-bit logits). The
   per-seed range 0.291–0.473 (15 % CV at 0.36 mean) is consistent
   with members living on different basins. The model-soup recipe
   assumes shared basin — see Wortsman 2022 §3.1, the "permutation
   alignment" extension in Ainsworth et al. 2023 ("Git Re-Basin"),
   and the negative result here.

2. **The Session 1 +55 % was inside the N=55 noise floor.** Session 1's
   journal flagged "95 % CI on the recovery rate is roughly ±13 pp"
   on the N=55 test fold. The +55 % relative (0.564 vs 0.364) was at
   the edge of significance on that fold. The production hard-200 +
   hard-1000 corpus has truth_decodes_total = 8853 + 28104 = 36,957
   — three orders of magnitude larger — and shows no signal in any
   direction. The Session 1 effect size was likely smaller than
   reported, but in a direction the larger sample cannot resolve from
   zero either.

Distinguishing (1) from (2) requires the more expensive Form A
output-space A/B (run all 8 forward passes, average logits, eval on
the same hard-200 + hard-1000 corpus). That experiment is NOT
performed in this session and was specifically deprioritized by the
brief.

## Surprises and architectural takeaways

1. **Model-soup is not a free lunch on from-scratch ensembles.** The
   Wortsman 2022 claim of "improves accuracy without increasing
   inference time" is contingent on fine-tuning from a shared init.
   Future ensemble work on small CNNs should EITHER use a shared
   pre-trained backbone (then the soup recipe applies) OR pay the 8×
   inference cost for output-space averaging.

2. **+82 % elapsed at unchanged architecture is a real signal.** This
   isn't measurement noise — it's a 1300-second wall-clock difference
   on the same 1063-WAV corpus, deterministic across two runs. Any
   change to the OSD ranker that slows downstream decode by this
   much is a non-starter regardless of recall metrics. Future eval-
   blob comparisons should include elapsed-time as a hard gate
   alongside the metric table.

3. **The Session 1 N=55 test fold was undersized for production
   decision-making.** With 95 % CI ±13 pp on the test recall, the
   reported +55 % relative was barely outside noise. The Phase A
   audit's "SESSION-1-COMPLETE-A/B-PENDING" re-label was correct
   ex-ante; this session is the corresponding ex-post verification.

4. **Production weights restored.** No code, weight-blob, or behavioral
   change leaks from this session into main. The two committed
   artifacts are the averaging script (a new tool, no runtime impact)
   and this journal.

## Reproducibility

```sh
# 1. Retrain the N=8 no-bootstrap ensemble
cd training/neural_osd
.venv/bin/python train_ensemble.py \
  --no-bootstrap --outdir session2 --ckpt-prefix ensemble_nb \
  --data /path/to/2026-05-31-hb-064-dia-osd-session1/trajectories.jsonl

# 2. Produce the averaged blob
.venv/bin/python average_ensemble_weights.py \
  --inputs session2/ensemble_nb_seed_42.pt session2/ensemble_nb_seed_43.pt \
           session2/ensemble_nb_seed_44.pt session2/ensemble_nb_seed_45.pt \
           session2/ensemble_nb_seed_46.pt session2/ensemble_nb_seed_47.pt \
           session2/ensemble_nb_seed_48.pt session2/ensemble_nb_seed_49.pt \
  --output ../../pancetta-ft8/assets/neural_osd_weights_ensemble.bin

# 3. Baseline eval (current production weights)
cd ../..
cargo build --release -p pancetta-research --bin eval --bin compare
./target/release/eval --mode ft8 \
  --tier curated-hard-200,curated-hard-1000 \
  --fp-filter-baselines research/baselines/ft8 \
  --output research/scorecards/sweep/hb194-session2-baseline.json

# 4. Swap to ensemble blob and rerun
cp pancetta-ft8/assets/neural_osd_weights.bin pancetta-ft8/assets/neural_osd_weights.bin.baseline
cp pancetta-ft8/assets/neural_osd_weights_ensemble.bin pancetta-ft8/assets/neural_osd_weights.bin
touch pancetta-ft8/assets/neural_osd_weights.bin
cargo build --release -p pancetta-research --bin eval
./target/release/eval --mode ft8 \
  --tier curated-hard-200,curated-hard-1000 \
  --fp-filter-baselines research/baselines/ft8 \
  --output research/scorecards/sweep/hb194-session2-ensemble.json

# 5. Bootstrap-CI compare
./target/release/compare \
  research/scorecards/sweep/hb194-session2-baseline.json \
  research/scorecards/sweep/hb194-session2-ensemble.json

# 6. Restore production blob
cp pancetta-ft8/assets/neural_osd_weights.bin.baseline pancetta-ft8/assets/neural_osd_weights.bin
```

Total wall-clock: ~75 minutes (8 s training + ~50 minutes eval × 2 +
build + compare).
