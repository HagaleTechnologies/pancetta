# Batch 55 — sparse-signal auto_passband re-measure

**Date**: 2026-06-09
**Branch**: `iter/2026-06-09-batch-55`
**Status**: MEASUREMENT BATCH — auto_passband line CLOSED with definitive shelve

## Headline

Batch 53 hypothesized that auto_passband's -1693 TPs on hard-200 was driven by signal-dense slots: the 95th-percentile-of-smoothed-spectrum peak estimator picks up real signals (not noise floor), narrowing the detected passband to exclude bins containing other real signals. Batch 55 tests by re-measuring on a synthetic sparse-signal corpus (50 slots, 1-3 signals/slot at SNRs -22 to -10 dB).

**Result: byte-identical** — Δ TPs = 0, Δ FPs = 0, byte-identical recall 0.6869 in both configs. The mechanism is **structurally inert** on sparse: with so few signal bins, the 95th-percentile threshold falls below the noise floor, so the leftmost/rightmost bins clearing it expand the detected passband to ~the full WG range — i.e., the auto-detected passband ≈ the default passband, so downstream behavior is byte-identical.

**Combined finding** (Batch 53 + Batch 55):
- Dense corpus: mechanism is HARMFUL (-1693 TPs / -31.9% recall on hard-200)
- Sparse corpus: mechanism is INERT (±0 TPs)
- No measured operating regime where it's net-positive.

The dense-band hypothesis was structurally correct but **the conclusion is reversed**: the mechanism doesn't have a "sweet-spot regime" between dense and sparse. It's either harmful or inert. **Definitive shelve** — auto_passband stays default-OFF permanently.

**Cumulative recovery (Batches 33-55)**: +369 TPs (unchanged — measurement batch, no ships).

Synthetic sparse corpus: 50 slots × (1-3 signals/slot, round-robin), 99 plant signals total.
- Per-signal SNR: drawn from [-22, -10] dB in 2500 Hz BW
- Per-signal audio freq: random in [500, 2400] Hz (modulator cap is 2456.25)
- Per-signal amplitude: random ±3 dB scaling to mimic capture-effect variation

|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| baseline (auto_passband OFF) | 145 | 68 | 77 | 0.6869 | 51.1s |
| auto_passband ON | 145 | 68 | 77 | 0.6869 | 52.0s |

Both configs decoded EXACTLY the same 68 TPs out of 99 plants (68.7% recall) — and the same 77 FPs. The decoder's output is **byte-identical** with auto_passband on vs off on this corpus.

## Why byte-identical on sparse

The auto_passband mechanism's three-step heuristic (smooth → 95th percentile → threshold = peak - DELTA) is designed for the assumption "the spectrum is dominated by noise floor with a few signal peaks." On sparse:

1. 95% of bins ARE noise floor.
2. The 95th percentile of the smoothed spectrum lands at the noise floor + tiny signal contribution from the few signals' broad sidelobes.
3. Threshold = (near noise floor) - DELTA = significantly below noise floor.
4. The leftmost/rightmost bins clearing the threshold span the entire WG window.
5. The auto-detected passband ≈ the default WG passband.
6. Downstream decoder behavior is byte-identical.

The 95th percentile approach is meant to **pick the highest signal lobe** when signals exist, then back off by DELTA to set a threshold ABOVE the noise floor that still includes the real band. On sparse, the 95th percentile is NOT a signal lobe; it's noise floor + bias.

## Why -1693 TPs on dense

On hard-200's dense slots:
1. 95% of bins contain signal lobes from one of the many simultaneous transmissions.
2. The 95th percentile of the smoothed spectrum lands at a real signal peak.
3. Threshold = (real signal peak) - DELTA = ABOVE noise floor but BELOW many other real signal peaks.
4. The leftmost/rightmost bins clearing the threshold span only the densest signal cluster.
5. The auto-detected passband is NARROWER than the default WG, excluding bins containing other real signals.
6. Decoder skips those bins → -1693 TPs.

## Strategic implication

**Close hb-NNN-style auto_passband as a graduation candidate.** The mechanism is fundamentally a "find the signal band" heuristic that requires a signal-band-vs-noise-band distinction. Pancetta's operating environment (HF amateur band, typically 10-50 concurrent signals across a 2 kHz audio passband) is signal-dense by definition. Even on sparse synthetic the mechanism can't find a productive operating point.

**Default-OFF is the permanent stance.** The mechanism stays wired (zero cost when OFF) but is no longer a candidate for default-ON.

The "faithful but inapplicable" family grows further. Auto_passband joins the cohort of peer-decoder mechanisms whose underlying assumptions don't survive pancetta's corpus + stack:
- hb-242 partial Costas (extra noise candidates eat into pancetta's cap)
- wide-lag baseline (no synergy with pancetta's downstream stack)
- three-stage sync cascade (pancetta's hb-044 already provides Stage 2 precision)
- npre2 OSD preprocessing (regime where it helps is itself a regression)
- 4th-pass-after-a7 (a7 doesn't surface fresh callsigns on pancetta's corpus)
- hb-237 cross-sequence A7 (corpus lacks operator's QSO continuity)
- **auto_passband (no operating regime where it's net-positive)** ← Batch 55 addition

**8 confirmed examples now.** The pattern is clean: peer-decoder mechanisms that pancetta's combination of (a) existing stack, (b) corpus characteristics, and (c) graduation gates rejects 50%+ of the time. The clean-room extraction process is working as designed — it surfaces candidates AND surfaces the why-it-doesn't-work explanation, both equally valuable.
