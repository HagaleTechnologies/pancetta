### Batch 75b — the probe defect and its correction

The sweep's `fourth_pass_after_a7` row came back byte-identical to baseline.
A wiring check showed why: the decoder gates the mechanism on
`a7_enabled && fourth_pass_after_a7_enabled` (decoder.rs:1553) and
`a7_enabled` defaults to **false** — the sweep row never engaged the
mechanism. Byte-identical-by-construction, not measured.

Corrected A/B (`batch75b_fourth_pass_a7.rs`, a7-ON baseline vs a7-ON +
fourth-pass, same corpus/truth):

| Label | Decodes | TPs | Δ TPs | FPs | Δ FPs | Precision |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| a7 ON (baseline) | 51175 | 37842 | +0 | 13333 | +0 | 0.7395 |
| a7 ON + fourth_pass ON | 51412 | 37865 | +23 | 13547 | +214 | 0.7365 |

fourth_pass is the only mechanism of the six with a real TP gain — but at
~9 FPs per TP, and it only exists downstream of a7, which is itself
default-OFF.

**Side-finding (free measurement)**: `a7_enabled` alone on raw_530_full =
**+0 TPs / +3,613 FPs**. Same shape as the Batch 72 OSD-depth story —
a recall mechanism tuned on hard corpora that only manufactures FPs on
realistic traffic. a7 is already default-OFF; this is confirmation, not a
change, but it should weigh against any future a7 default-ON proposal.

### Note on three_stage_sync_cascade

The one interesting trade: −964 FPs (−9.9% of all FPs) for −69 TPs (−0.18%),
+1.6 precision points. Fails the pre-registered rule (TP-negative), and the
Batch 72 osd ship already took the cheap precision win, so this stays
shelved — but it is the only sync-stage mechanism observed to buy precision
at near-zero recall cost. If a future operational mode wants a
max-precision profile (e.g., unattended logging), this flag is the first
candidate to revisit.

## Methodological lessons

1. **Verify flag reachability before believing byte-identical results.**
   Three rows came back identical to baseline; only a code-path check
   distinguished "engaged but inert" (cycle smoothing, gaussian ramp) from
   "never engaged" (fourth_pass). The latter would have been recorded as a
   false confirm-shelve without the check.
2. **Mechanism-label drift**: the probe's doc-comment labels "hb-230" /
   "hb-226" follow the Batch 47-49 clean-room journals, which diverge from
   the bank's hb-230/hb-226 headings (different mechanisms). Journals and
   bank updates here reference config-flag names, which are unambiguous.
3. (Carried from Batch 77, same day) Small pancetta-truth TP deltas keep
   shrinking to zero under neutral truth; the pre-registered decision rule
   + ft8_lib truth made every verdict here mechanical.
