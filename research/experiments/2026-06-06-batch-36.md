### B4 — mp=2-only TPs by SNR bucket

The 32 mp=2-only TPs, bucketed:

| SNR bucket | count |
|---|---:|
| **≥-5 dB** | **16 (50%)** |
| -10 .. -5 | 8 |
| -15 .. -10 | 3 |
| -19 .. -15 | 3 |
| -22 .. -19 | 1 |
| <-22 | 1 |

**Half the mp=2 gain is at strong SNR (≥-5 dB).** This is *not* a
sensitivity story — at strong SNR pancetta already decodes the
loudest signal cleanly. What mp=2 does is run a second decode pass
on the residual after subtracting the loudest signal, exposing
quieter neighbors that the first pass couldn't separate. This is
the **capture-effect recovery mechanism** quantified at corpus scale.

Implication: hb-218 (capture-effect joint decoding, plan-sized) is
attacking the same mechanism mp=2 is helping with. Batch 34's
hb-218 sizing was ~1058 truths blocked by ±25 Hz neighbors;
mp=2 recovers ~16 of those at strong SNR (mp=2 is a cheaper but
coarser hb-218 substitute). hb-218 remains worth its plan — the
remaining ~1042 capture-locked truths sit beyond mp=2's reach
(they need genuine joint decoding, not just subtract-and-retry).

## C-track — slot-edge sync recon

### C1 — Costas sync t0 range (code-read)

`pancetta-ft8/src/decoder.rs:1921`:

```rust
for t0 in 0..=max_time_step {
    // ...
}
```

`max_time_step = spectrogram.num_steps.saturating_sub(msg_span + 1)`.

**t0 is structurally non-negative.** A signal whose actual start
falls before the WAV's first sample (negative-dt in jt9's truth)
cannot be located by the current sync. This is the floor on
slot-edge coverage.

To recover negative-dt signals, the decoder either:
- (a) pre-pads input with silence so dt becomes positive (Batch 35 A
  and Batch 36 C3 both demonstrate this loses more than it gains)
- (b) extends the spectrogram generation to include a buffer of
  prior-slot tail samples (would need coordinator-level buffering
  and a sync that can iterate negative conceptual t0)

(b) is a decoder-internal change — non-trivial; not within batch scope.

### C2 — dt bucket recall

Full hard-200, default config:

| dt_s bucket | truth | recovered | recall |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| ≥2.0 | 3908 | 2452 | 62.7% |
| 1.5..2.0 | 1694 | 1055 | 62.3% |
| 1.0..1.5 | 1375 | 819 | 59.6% |
| 0.5..1.0 | 166 | 93 | 56.0% |
| 0..0.5 | 334 | 170 | 50.9% |
| **-0.5..0** | **1293** | **651** | **50.3%** |
| **-1.0..-0.5** | 64 | 13 | 20.3% |
| **-1.5..-1.0** | 13 | 0 | **0%** |
| **<-1.5** | 6 | 0 | **0%** |

**Critical correction to the Batch 34 memory**: the "1376 slot-edge
truths" headline conflated *all* truths with dt<0 into a single
bucket. The actual distribution shows:

- 1293 of 1376 negative-dt truths sit at dt ∈ [-0.5, 0). At this
  bucket, recall is **50.3%** — statistically indistinguishable
  from dt ∈ [0, 0.5) at 50.9%. There is no sync cliff for these.
- Only **83 truths** sit at dt < -0.5, where the real cliff is
  (20.3% / 0% / 0% recall).

**Slot-edge sync expansion headroom: ~66-83 truths** (the worst-
case fix would recover all 83 truths at dt < -0.5; realistic fix
maybe recovers 50-66 of them). **Much smaller than mp=2's +32, and
2-3× smaller than hb-218's 1042-truth capture-effect headroom.**

The dt<-1 cliff (19 truths) is genuinely structural and cannot be
recovered without (b)-style decoder change. The dt -1..-0.5
bucket (64 truths) is partial-recovery territory.

### C3 — silence-pad variant sweep

First 50 WAVs, baseline-cfg decode of `samples` vs padded decode of
`[zeros × N; samples]`:

| pad | gained TPs | lost TPs | net |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 0.5s | 14 | 50 | **-36** |
| 1.0s | 19 | 95 | **-76** |
| 1.5s | 2 | 1196 | **-1194** |

**All variants net-negative.** 0.5s pad is the least bad but still
costs 36 TPs more than it recovers. 1.5s is catastrophic — padding
shifts every signal's apparent dt forward by 1.5s, pushing many
signals' Costas sync alignment beyond the spectrogram's window
ability to find them (or shifting them past the search bounds).

**Silence padding is fundamentally the wrong slot-edge fix.** The
right approach is decoder-internal sync extension (path (b) from
C1) — which is out of scope for this batch.

## Strategic implications for the next batch

Post-Batch-36, the next-batch landscape is:

**Option A — hb-218 capture-effect joint decoding** (plan-sized, ~1042
truths headroom): still the largest single mechanism. mp=2's
strong-SNR gain confirms the corpus has the capture-effect signal at
scale. Worth its plan.

**Option B — multipass mp=3 incremental** (+6 TPs at +50% extra
wall-clock): characterized clean. Not worth shipping on its own.
Could be paired with a later osd-depth bump if some hardware tier
above Fast emerges. **No batch action needed.**

**Option C — slot-edge sync expansion** (~66-83 truths headroom,
non-trivial decoder change): downgraded from "medium leverage"
(initial planning estimate based on 1376 number) to "small leverage"
(actual 83 truths). Still real, but no longer the second-place
strategic option. The mechanism would need a decoder-internal
pre-slot-buffer + extended Costas sync.

**Recommendation for Batch 37**: hb-218 is now the unambiguous
next-batch headline. The mp=2 / SNR-bucketing data from B4 gives
hb-218 a corpus-validated foundation: 1058 strong-SNR
capture-locked truths exist; mp=2 picks off ~16 of them via
brute-force residual decoding; a proper joint decoder addresses
the rest. Slot-edge can be deferred indefinitely.
