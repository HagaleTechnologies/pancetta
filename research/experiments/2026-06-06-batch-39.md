## AA — Anomaly characterization

### AA1 — Sub-bucket by `|Δsnr|` magnitude

| Δsnr (truth-louder) | count |
|---|---:|
| 3-6 dB louder | 63 |
| 6-12 dB louder | 105 |
| 12-20 dB louder | 62 |
| 20+ dB louder | 8 |

Median bucket is 6-12 dB. If the anomaly were a real priority bug,
the 12-20 dB and 20+ dB cases should be especially suspicious
(it's hard to imagine sync prioritizing a signal 20 dB weaker). 70
truths in these "very louder" buckets ought to be ringing alarm bells.

### AA2 — FFT-energy(truth.freq) vs FFT-energy(neighbor.freq)

For top-50 louder-missed, computed Hann-windowed DFT energy in ±50 Hz
band around `truth.freq_hz` vs `neighbor.freq_hz`, summed across the
15s WAV. Compared to jt9's claim that truth is louder by `|Δsnr|`:

| `10·log10(truth_energy / neighbor_energy)` | count | % |
|---|---:|---:|
| <-3 dB (truth quieter!) | 1 | 2% |
| -3..0 dB (truth ~ neighbor) | 18 | 36% |
| 0..3 dB (truth slightly louder) | 30 | 60% |
| 3..9 dB | 1 | 2% |
| 9+ dB | 0 | 0% |

**47/50 (94%) had truth NOT measurably louder than neighbor in
pancetta's spectrum view.** jt9 claimed these truths were
3-20+ dB louder; the FFT-energy probe shows they're within ±1 dB.

This is a measurement-quality finding: **jt9's per-decode SNR
estimate has substantial noise** at the regime these capture-locked
pairs live in. The "louder-missed" anomaly from Batch 37 AA3 is
largely an artifact of this noise, not a real pancetta priority bug.

Why might jt9's SNR be off? jt9 estimates SNR from local noise floor
relative to the decoded signal's tone strength. In a capture-effect
pair, the two signals share spectrum cells; jt9 measures each as if
the other weren't there. The "louder" claim is jt9 saying "this
decoder thinks tone A is louder than tone B locally" — but those
local estimates ignore the cross-signal contamination.

## BB — Mechanism probes

### BB1 — V3 force-decode (relax=-3.0, window=12)

Ran pancetta on all 122 WAVs holding louder-missed truths with
`joint_residual_sync_relax_db = -3.0` (more aggressive than Batch 38's
-2.0) and `joint_residual_sync_window_bins = 12` (wider than default 8).

**0/238 louder-missed truths recovered.**

Even maximum V3 relaxation doesn't surface a single louder-missed
truth. Combined with AA2's finding that these truths aren't actually
louder, this is consistent: V3 looks for sub-threshold sync peaks in
the localized window, and there's no decodable signal there.

### BB2 — Neighbor-mask probe (notch ±60 Hz around neighbor.freq)

For top-20 louder-missed, applied a bandstop FIR filter notching
±60 Hz around `neighbor.freq_hz`, then re-decoded the filtered WAV.
If the neighbor's energy was occluding the truth, removing it should
surface the truth.

**0/20 recovered.**

The truths don't surface even when the neighbor's frequency band is
removed. This rules out "the neighbor's wideband energy is masking
the truth" — the truth signal genuinely isn't there at decodable
amplitude.

(Caveat: a 60 Hz notch around the neighbor would also notch the
truth in cases with `Δfreq < 60 Hz`, which is most of them. So
BB2 has limited reach — but the result is still informative for
cases where neighbor freq is well-separated.)

### CC1 — Synth 2-signal control

Generated two clean FT8 signals: A at 1000 Hz amplitude 1.0
("K1ABC W2DEF FN42"), B at 1015 Hz amplitude 0.5 (≈6 dB quieter,
"W2DEF K1ABC -10"). Δfreq=15 Hz. Added light AWGN. Decoded.

```
A (louder, 1000 Hz) decoded: true
B (quieter, 1015 Hz, -6dB) decoded: true
```

Pancetta correctly decodes **both** in clean conditions. The synth
case proves there's no broken sync-priority logic that would cause
the louder of a pair to be systematically missed. **If the real
corpus exhibits anything that looks like "louder missed", it's not
because pancetta's sync prefers quieter signals.**
