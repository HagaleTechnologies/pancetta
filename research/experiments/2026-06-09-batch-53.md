## What was measured

### Probe A — Batch 52 mechanisms on hard-200

Config: `max_decode_passes = 2, ldpc_iterations = 200`. Reference baseline: **5301 TPs**.

| Config | Decodes | TPs | Δ vs baseline | Precision | Elapsed |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| baseline (both OFF) | 7082 | **5301** | 0 | 0.7485 | 435.2s |
| `auto_passband_enabled` ON | 5094 | **3608** | **−1693** | 0.7083 | 454.1s |
| `cross_sequence_a7_enabled` ON (no seeds) | 7082 | **5301** | **0** | 0.7485 | 447.6s |
| both ON | 5095 | 3608 | −1693 | 0.7081 | 454.9s |

**Findings**:

1. **auto_passband ON costs −1693 TPs (−31.9% recall)** on hard-200. The mechanism is "faithful but wrong regime": the 95th-percentile-of-smoothed-spectrum peak estimator picks up real signals (the band is dense), not noise floor, so the threshold-derived passband EXCLUDES bins containing real signals. The peak quantile assumes a noise-floor-dominated spectrum; hard-200 violates that assumption.

2. **cross_seq ON with empty seeds is byte-identical to baseline** (decodes 7082→7082, TPs 5301→5301). The decoder's defense-in-depth empty-seed early-return guard works as designed. The coordinator-side flag plumbed through Session 3 inflicts ZERO cost when there's no prior-slot context.

3. **Both ON ≈ auto_passband ON alone**. As expected: cross-seq contributes 0 in independent-slot mode.

### Probe B — Cross-sequence A7 end-to-end on chrono_replay_mini33

Walks 33 sequenced slots in `slot_index` order. With cross_seq ON, seeds for slot N+1 are harvested from slot N's `from_callsign`/`to_callsign` (uppercased, dedup, raw — no trust-set gate, intentionally worst-case).

| Config | Decodes | TPs | cs_emits | Δ TPs | Precision | Elapsed |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| cross_seq OFF (baseline) | 902 | 690 | 0 | 0 | 0.7650 | 62.0s |
| cross_seq ON | 989 | **690** | **87** | **0** | 0.6977 | 62.5s |

**Findings**:

1. **The cross-sequence consumer fires** — `cs_emits = 87` decodes emitted across 33 slots that the standard pipeline missed. Mechanism is alive end-to-end.
2. **Zero of those 87 are TPs**. Every cross-sequence-recovered decode on this corpus is a FP. The mechanism is **FP-amplifying** when seeds bypass the trust gate — exactly the risk hb-237 spec §"FP risk" flagged.
3. **Precision crashes 0.7650 → 0.6977** (−9% relative). The probe path bypasses the production coordinator's `record_decoded_trusted` gate; in production, seeds are filtered through the `CallsignContinuityFilter` (hb-062 trust set). This probe characterizes the worst-case "what if the trust gate fails" state — it's bad.

The 87 FPs are not random noise: the consumer specifically generates a7 templates ROOTED at each seed callsign, so when a real signal is decoded with mis-attributed callsign, the consumer produces structurally-plausible FPs. This is the FP-amplification cascade the spec warned about.

**Implication**: the trust-gated invocation path is non-negotiable for any future cross-sequence ship. The current coordinator wire (`record_decoded_trusted` → `get_a7_candidates_with_parity`) is the right shape. Default-OFF is the correct production stance until either (a) a corpus exercising the trust gate validates net-positive TP recovery, or (b) a tighter post-emit FP filter graduates.

### Probe C — LLR whitening on hard_1000 (GRADUATION DECISION)

| Config | Decodes | TPs | Δ TPs | FPs | Δ FPs | Precision | Elapsed |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| baseline (whitening OFF) | 22365 | **16365** | 0 | 6000 | 0 | **0.7317** | 2201.0s |
| whitening ON | 21656 | **16369** | **+4** | 5287 | **−713** | **0.7559** | 2229.7s |

**Findings**:

1. **TPs lift survives at 5× corpus scale** (+4 on hard_1000, scaled from +2 on hard_200). Sub-linear (linear scaling would predict +10) but consistently positive.
2. **Precision lift is robust**: 0.7317 → 0.7559 (+3.3% relative), matching the Batch 50 hard-200 finding (+2.7%) almost exactly.
3. **The dominant effect is FP reduction**: **−713 FPs** (6000 → 5287). For an autonomous-station deployment, fewer FPs is operationally more valuable than the marginal TP lift — every FP risks a wasted TX cycle / wrong-callsign QSO attempt.

**Decision**: GRADUATE to default-ON. Flipped `llr_whitening_enabled: false → true` in `Ft8Config::default()` and updated the regression test `default_config_keeps_whitening_off` → `default_config_keeps_whitening_on`. The flip is the active production stance for Batch 53.

This is the FIRST default-ON graduation from the implementer-thread waves (Batches 48-52 shipped all 12 mechanisms opt-in). The graduation criterion: TPs ↑ AND precision ↑ on a corpus 5× larger than the discovery corpus.

## Strategic implications

1. **Batch 52's two opt-in mechanisms both confirmed-negative on their respective corpora.** This is a **clean default-OFF decision** by Batch 52 — the cost of going default-ON would have been a −31.9% recall hit on hard-200 (auto_passband) or a −9% precision drop on chronological corpora (cross-seq A7 without trust gate). The default-OFF discipline introduced over the implementer-thread waves is paying off.

2. **Auto-passband joins the "faithful but inapplicable" family**: same shape as Batch 49 hb-242 (partial Costas), Batch 49 wide-lag, Batch 51 three-stage cascade, Batch 51 npre2 (at depth=1), Batch 52 npre2 (at depth=3). These are peer-decoder mechanisms that pancetta's existing stack either renders inert or actively degrades. **Six confirmed examples**; pancetta's bootstrap-CI graduation discipline is catching them all.

3. **Cross-sequence A7's value depends entirely on the trust gate.** The bare consumer is FP-amplifying. Production-safe deployment requires the coordinator's `record_decoded_trusted` filter (hb-062 trust set integration) to be active. This is **already** how Session 3 wired it — but the measurement makes the "trust gate is load-bearing" claim concrete.

4. **The peer-decoder spec-sweep apparatus is producing a high "ship default-OFF" rate**. Of the ~12 mechanisms shipped opt-in from Batches 48-52, ~6 are confirmed inapplicable / regressive on pancetta's corpus. That's a 50% inert rate — high enough that future implementer-thread waves should bias toward MEASUREMENT-FIRST (build the probe before/during impl) to surface the inert-on-this-corpus cases earlier.

5. **LLR whitening graduated to default-ON** — first default-ON graduation from the implementer-thread waves. **Cumulative recovery now +369 TPs** (was +365; +4 from this graduation). The graduation policy held: a 5×-corpus hard_1000 validation gated the flip, and both TPs and precision survived.
