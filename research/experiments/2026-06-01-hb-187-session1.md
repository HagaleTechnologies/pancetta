## Headline

**`facebook/wav2vec2-base` loads on MPS in <1s, embeds 12kHz FT8 audio
resampled to 16kHz at ~145 ms per ~13s WAV, and produces pooled
embeddings with measurable structure (pairwise cosine mean 0.698, PC1
explains 45%, top-5 cumulative 92%, effective rank ≈ 4.7 across 10
samples).** No collapse, no random noise, no NaN, no auth issue.

Decision: **PROCEED to Session 2.** All three structure gates passed.

## Hypothesis (re-statement, hb-187 / F1)

> Take a pretrained self-supervised speech encoder (Wav2Vec2-base,
> ~95M params, MIT-licensed). Resample FT8 audio to 16 kHz, feed it
> raw, take last-layer activations. Train a thin adapter head to
> emit 174 codeword LLRs. Frozen encoder, only the head trains.
> Defensible prior: ICASSP 2025 paper showing self-supervised speech
> features transfer to narrowband audio including FT4/FT8 in 7/9
> task families.

## Session 1 scope

This was a **feasibility + design** session — not a training run, not
an integration. Three concrete questions:

1. Can we even load Wav2Vec2 locally on the M2 MPS device?
2. Do the embeddings have non-degenerate structure on real FT8 audio?
3. Is the latency in a plausible deployment envelope?

If all three → write Session 2 design spec, hand off.

## Method

`training/neural_osd/hb187_wav2vec_probe.py`:

1. Load 10 WAVs from `~/.pancetta/recordings/ft8_20260530_*.wav`
   (today's K5ARH capture, 2066 WAVs available).
2. Each WAV: int16 → float32, peak-normalize, polyphase resample
   12kHz → 16kHz (up=4, down=3).
3. Feed through `Wav2Vec2FeatureExtractor` + `Wav2Vec2Model.from_pretrained('facebook/wav2vec2-base')` on MPS.
4. Take `last_hidden_state` (shape `[1, T_seq, 768]`), mean-pool over
   time → `[768]` per WAV.
5. Compute pairwise cosine, PCA on the 10×768 stack, effective rank.

Decision rule (conservative — calibrated to flag both collapse and noise):
- pairwise cosine mean in [0.3, 0.95] (non-collapsed, non-random)
- PC1 explained variance < 0.80 (not single-direction dominated)
- PC1 explained variance > 0.15 (above noise floor; random would
  be ≈ 1/N for top components)

Median (warm): **144.5 ms / WAV** (~13s of audio).

Per-candidate scaling (Session 2 deployment uses 3.16s windows):
- 3.16s × (144.5 ms / 13s) ≈ **35 ms per candidate, unbatched**.
- 50 candidates per slot unbatched: ~1750 ms (over the 500ms budget).
- 50 candidates batched: expected ~300 ms (per HF perf table batch
  scaling on M2 Wav2Vec2-base). Session 2 kill-switch will measure
  this empirically.

### Embedding structure

```
Pairwise cosine: mean=0.6977  std=0.1687  min=0.3290  max=0.9959
PCA top-5 EVR: [0.454, 0.164, 0.141, 0.084, 0.079]
PCA top-5 cumulative: [0.454, 0.618, 0.759, 0.842, 0.922]
Effective rank (entropy-based, top 5): 4.724
```

| Gate | Threshold | Observed | Pass? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Non-collapsed | cos in [0.3, 0.95] | 0.698 | yes |
| Non-dominated | PC1 < 0.80 | 0.454 | yes |
| Above noise | PC1 > 0.15 | 0.454 | yes |

All three pass. Embeddings are **structurally rich** — the encoder is
not collapsing to a constant, not producing noise, and not putting all
variance on a single direction. The cos-min of 0.33 between two WAVs
shows the encoder distinguishes audio content; the cos-max of 0.996
between two adjacent WAVs (`152428.wav` and `152443.wav`, 15s apart on
the same band) shows it recognizes near-identical spectral conditions.

This is exactly the "non-trivial but coherent" structure we'd want
before training a head on top.

### Interpretation

The 10 samples used here are sequential 15-second slots from the same
K5ARH band-pass session. Many adjacent slots have near-identical
ambient noise spectrum and overlapping decode populations, which
explains why cos is concentrated around 0.7 (high but not 1.0). The
fact that PC1 only accounts for 45% (not 95%) means the encoder is
*differentiating* even between similar slots — likely along occupancy
density, SNR, and frequency-distribution axes.

We did not label any WAVs in this session; structural probes only.
Session 2 will introduce ground-truth bits and train the head.

## Disposition

**PROCEED to Session 2.** All gates passed. Wav2Vec2 is alive on MPS,
the embeddings carry signal, and the latency story is plausible (if
tight) with batching. The design spec at
`docs/superpowers/specs/2026-06-01-hb-187-wav2vec2-design.md` lays out
the head architecture (frozen Wav2Vec2 + 574K-param MLP → 174 LLRs),
training corpus mix (synth + hard-1000 + capture + hard-200 OOD held
out), four kill-switches (LLR correlation, latency, train cost, OOD
regression), and the A/B integration plan against the production
decoder.

## Risks carried into Session 2

1. **Latency overrun.** If batched-50 inference exceeds 500 ms on M2,
   the sidecar deployment path is dead and we'd need ONNX → Rust
   (deferred to Session 3+). Session 2 kill-switch (2) measures this
   before training time is sunk.
2. **OOD regression.** hb-064 S2 burned us with -135 novels from a
   smaller neural model overfitting the train pool. Session 2
   kill-switch (4) gates on hard-200 OOD specifically.
3. **Window size.** Per-candidate 3.16s windows may not give the
   encoder enough context to exploit Costas-array repetitions. The
   spec includes a stretch ablation comparing per-candidate vs
   per-slot inputs.

## Estimated cumulative-composite impact (rough)

Foundation-model swings are big. If the head graduates at
hard-200 +20 rec / hard-1000 +30 rec (the spec's graduation gate),
that's a **~+0.005 composite swing**, which would be the second-largest
single-iteration delta in project history (after hb-079's
iterative-subtract). If it shelves, the cost was ~1 working day
spread over 3 sessions and ~$0 of compute.

Risk-adjusted expected value: high. Going in.
