## Strategic implications

1. **LLR whitening is the first Batch 49/50 mechanism with a net-positive on hard-200.** Small but real (+2 TPs / +2.7% precision). Pancetta is the first FT8 decoder shipping it.
2. **4th-pass-after-a7 is corpus-inert** on hard-200 because a7 isn't producing new callsigns from decoded ones. Needs a corpus with more single-callsign-only decodes whose tail messages could be a7-recovered.
3. **Per-candidate freq tracker adds noise on static-HF signals.** Most hard-200 WAVs are short signal paths without drift; the tracker compensates for drift that isn't there. Should test on mobile / aurora / chirpy-radio corpora.
4. **Cross-sequence A7 is Session 1 complete.** Session 2 (decoder consumption of a7 hints, ~350-450 LOC in pancetta-ft8) is the next priority for the hb-237 line.

## Process learnings

1. **Worktree discipline works at scale.** 4 parallel agents, no contention, easy cherry-pick. Memory entry `feedback_implementer_thread_worktree_discipline` validated.
2. **Honest deferrals are wisdom.** Every agent shipped a "Session 1 of N" scope. The cross-sequence A7 cache without decoder consumption is the clearest example — useful infrastructure even though recall lift is unmeasured until Session 2.
3. **Probe-side discipline matters too.** The original measurement agent (Batch 50) returned a "running, waiting for completion" report and exited without actually waiting for the probe. I ran the probe myself to get results. Lesson for measurement-agent prompts: include explicit "do not exit until results are committed".
