# hb-093 — Per-position residual SNR pre-decode gate (SHELVED)

**Date**: 2026-06-01
**Branch**: iter/2026-06-01-hb-093
**Status**: SHELVED — elapsed savings ceiling (~2-3%) below the 10%
graduation bar; gate works as designed but optimization headroom on
joint_pair_retry is too small.

## Hypothesis

Per mr-008 ideation: V3 (subtract-aware sync threshold relaxation) and
hb-088 (OSD-without-Costas) both SHELVED because sub-Costas / residual
candidate positions surfaced hundreds of noise candidates per WAV that
LDPC always-converges-on. The wasted work was substantial.

hb-093: before LDPC at any residual `joint_pair_retry_pass` candidate,
compute a per-position residual SNR estimate. If below a calibrated
threshold, SKIP LDPC. This is a GATING optimization — should reduce
elapsed without affecting recall.

## V3-doctrine two-part kill switch

Per `feedback_research_experiment_workflow.md`:

1. **Efficiency**: ≥30% of candidates filtered → PROCEED
2. **Decodability**: ≤2% of filtered candidates would have decoded → PROCEED

## Decision: SHELVE

Iter spec: "GRADUATE if any setting gives ≥10% elapsed drop with
composite ≥ baseline. SHELVE if no setting works."

The actual elapsed reduction ceiling is **~2.94%** (at the most
aggressive setting, which costs 10 recovered decodes). At the safest
setting (gate=−10 dB) the gate gives **−2.08%** elapsed with **zero**
recall loss — a real, clean optimization but **far below the 10% bar**.

Mechanism finding: joint_pair_retry on the post-multipass residual is a
small slice of total elapsed time (`coherent_subtract_and_repass` and
the pass-1 par_iter dominate). Even filtering 36.5% of pair_retry
candidates only saves ~2-3% of the total slot.

The plumbing is preserved at default-off (`residual_snr_gate_db: None`)
for future revisits when a different mechanism (e.g. dual-gate combining
this with hb-016 residual-energy stop, or applying the same gate INSIDE
`coherent_subtract_and_repass` step 4) might push the elapsed delta over
the bar.

## Lessons

1. **Diagnostic vs production gap**: diagnostic measured 36.5% candidate
   filter rate at −5 dB. That filter rate is real, but pair_retry
   candidates contribute only a small fraction of total elapsed —
   filtering 36% of 666 LDPC calls / WAV doesn't move the per-WAV wall
   clock by much when the pass-1 decode loop is doing the bulk of the
   LDPC work on the full sync_candidates list.

2. **Decoded-SNR floor (−3.7 dB) is real**: pair_retry decodes sit well
   above the noise candidates. The gate at any threshold from −5 to
   −10 dB cost ≤ 2 recovered decodes — the SNR proxy is well-calibrated
   for this task even if the elapsed savings are bounded.

3. **Future revisit path**: extending the gate to
   `coherent_subtract_and_repass` step 4 (which sees the same residual
   spectrogram and the same `par_estimate_snr_spectrogram` could be
   applied) would push the filter to a bigger LDPC count — the per-round
   candidate count is `max_sync_candidates` (200), much larger than the
   ~130/WAV pair_retry surface. That's the obvious next mechanism if a
   future iter wants to revisit.
