## Hypothesis

The decoder's two OSD knobs are the OSD depth (production: `Some(2)`) and
LDPC iterations cap (production: `25`, vs WSJT-X's `50`). Per hb-005:
(a) compare OSD-1 vs OSD-2 vs disabled on the corpus to quantify OSD-only
vs LDPC-only decodes; (b) raise LDPC iterations from 25 to 50 and check
whether more BP convergence substitutes for OSD-2 on the hard cases.

Also: this cycle pivoted **from hb-004**. The original plan was the
AP-survival gate sweep, but a Phase-1 audit showed `decode_window` calls
`decode_window_with_ap` with `ApContext::default()` — `ap_active=false`
in eval, so `MIN_SYNC_SCORE_FOR_AP` gating never fires. The hb-004
hypothesis assumes a wiring that the harness doesn't have; documented
as a deferred scoping question.

## Change

Production change: raised `LDPC_MAX_ITERATIONS` from 25 to 50 in
`pancetta-ft8/src/decoder.rs:41`. OSD depth stays at `Some(2)`
(the sweep showed OSD-3 explodes novel-but-not-confirmed decodes,
likely FPs; OSD-2 is the right safe upper bound).

Research infrastructure:
- `pancetta-research/src/decoder.rs` — `with_osd_depth(Option<u8>)` and
  `with_ldpc_iterations(usize)` builder methods.
- `pancetta-research/src/bin/eval.rs` — `--osd-depth` (accepts `none`,
  `0`, `1`, `2`, `3`) and `--ldpc-iters N` CLI flags.

| none | 50    | 0.4739  | 4064 |  752  | 0.2369    | 115.1   |
| 1    | 25    | 0.4719  | 4047 |  750  | 0.2359    | 119.6   |

| 2    | 25    | 0.4724  | 4051 |  868  | 0.2362    | 135.8   |

| 3    | 50    | 0.4748  | 4072 | 1207  | 0.2374    | 164.7   |

Key reads:
- **OSD's contribution to confirmed decodes is tiny.** Range across
  OSD ∈ {none, 1, 2, 3} at iters=25 is just 6 decodes (4045 → 4051).
  At iters=50, the range is 8 (4064 → 4072). OSD is not where the gap is.
- **OSD-3 explodes novel-but-unconfirmed decodes.** Going OSD-2 → OSD-3
  at iters=25 adds 0 recovered but +313 novel. These are almost
  certainly FPs (the parity gate ≤4 isn't tight enough at OSD-3's
  125K trials per candidate; hb-018 already flagged this).
- **LDPC iters 25 → 50 is the real win.** At OSD-2: +14 recovered,
  +2 novel — pure gain. Consistent +14-21 recovered at every OSD setting.

**Notably**: hard-1000 gained +64 recovered AND lost 54 novel. That's a
real signal — more LDPC iterations is converting fuzzy "novel" decodes
into confirmed truth-matches. The novel count getting smaller while
recovered grows is exactly the right direction for a quality
improvement.

**The 5-tier eval got FASTER**, not slower. More iterations per LDPC
call, but more successful BP convergence means fewer expensive OSD
fallbacks fire. Net: -3% wall-clock.

## Disposition

**WIN.** Production default `LDPC_MAX_ITERATIONS` raised from 25 to 50.
OSD depth unchanged (Some(2) stays). All five tiers either improved or
were unchanged. No regression flags. Composite +0.0008.

Sweep tooling lands as reusable infra (`--osd-depth`, `--ldpc-iters`).

## Learnings

- **OSD is not where the WSJT-X gap lives either.** Going from OSD-none
  to OSD-2 only nets 6 recovered decodes on hard-200 (0.15%). The
  module's documented "OSD-2 has high CRC-14 FP rate without
  additional validation" is partly true: OSD-2 *does* add 120 novel
  decodes vs OSD-none at iters=25, but only 6 of those align with jt9
  truth. OSD-3 makes it dramatically worse — +313 unconfirmed novels
  for zero additional recovered.

- **LDPC iters 25 → 50 is a quality knob masquerading as a sensitivity
  knob.** The headline gain is +0.0023 decode_rate on hard-1000, but
  the more interesting signal is the +64 recovered / -54 novel split:
  more BP convergence eliminates fuzzy decodes that don't match jt9.
  Pancetta's "novel" count dropped from 2779 to 2725 — slightly fewer
  unconfirmed claims, more confirmed ones.

- **More LDPC iterations are FASTER than expected.** Naively, 50 iters
  should be ~2× slower than 25 per LDPC call. But the BudgetTracker
  3000ms cap is per-WAV, not per-candidate; and OSD fallback (when BP
  doesn't converge) is ~5-10× slower than BP itself. Net: more BP
  convergence → fewer OSD calls → time drops 3% overall.

- **hb-004 (AP gate retune) needs scope work.** The eval's
  `decode_window` calls `decode_window_with_ap` with the default
  (empty) `ApContext`, so AP never fires during eval runs. The hb-004
  bank entry assumed AP was exercised — it isn't. Sweeping the gate
  would change nothing measurable. To make hb-004 meaningful, the
  harness would need to inject `my_call` (e.g., a callsign from the
  corpus's QSO traffic) into an ApContext. That's a separate
  meta-experiment with its own tradeoffs (biases the eval toward a
  specific operating callsign). Bank entry updated to flag this.

- **OSD-3 is shelved by the parity-gate ceiling.** hb-018 (OSD-3 with
  strengthened CRC validation) is now better-targeted: without a
  tighter FP filter than the current MAX_PARITY_ERRORS_FOR_OSD=4,
  OSD-3 produces +313 unconfirmed novels per 200 WAVs. The parity
  gate would need to drop to ≤2 or 3 for OSD-3 to be viable, and that
  may also kill OSD-2's contribution.

## Reproducing

```bash
# OSD × iters sweep on hard-200:
for OSD in none 1 2 3; do
    for ITERS in 25 50; do
        cargo run --release -p pancetta-research --bin eval -- \
            --tier curated-hard-200 --mode ft8 \
            --osd-depth $OSD --ldpc-iters $ITERS \
            --output research/scorecards/sweep/hard200-osd-${OSD}-iters-${ITERS}.json
    done
done

# Full 5-tier confirmation at winner:
cargo run --release -p pancetta-research --bin eval -- \
    --tier fixtures,synth-clean,curated-hard-200,curated-hard-1000,wild-50 \
    --mode ft8 --osd-depth 2 --ldpc-iters 50 \
    --output research/scorecards/osd-sweep.json
```
