## Why hb-237 stays shelved

Both truth sources (pancetta-without-cross_seq, ft8_lib) fail to confirm hb-237's recoveries. The mechanism produces ~8% conversion (seeds → emits) consistently across corpora, with ~0% TP rate against neutral truth.

Possible explanations:
1. **The cross_seq A7 implementation produces spurious matches at noise.** A7 enumerates templates rooted at seed callsigns; if the audio doesn't actually carry those templates, the LDPC decoder still occasionally finds a syntactically-valid match by chance.
2. **The truth gate prevents BAD callsigns from being seeded but doesn't prevent GOOD callsigns from generating spurious matches.** A real callsign passing the trust gate doesn't mean the spectrogram contains a signal at that callsign's templated audio frequency.
3. **The mechanism's intended regime requires operator-in-QSO context that even real recordings don't fully reproduce.** The operator's QSO partner replying is structurally different from random sequential band traffic.

Without operator verification (Phase 5 on-air work), we can't fully distinguish. The conservative + reproducible verdict: **default-OFF, well-tested wiring preserved, revisit when meatspace verification is feasible**.

## What the +1233 FPs mean for precision

Precision impact: 9077/13250 = 0.685 → 9077/14483 = 0.626. **−5.9 points absolute precision** (−8.6% relative). That's the operational cost of `cross_sequence_a7_enabled = true` on this corpus.

For Phase 5 autonomous-station deployment, this precision drop is significant. Even if hb-237 occasionally recovers a real signal that ft8_lib misses, the FP rate is unacceptable for an autonomous station that uses decodes to decide TX actions.
