## Diagnostic result — kill-switch failed

Run: `cargo run --release -p pancetta-research --example hb088_osd_without_costas_feasibility`

Method: for every missed truth in the top-20 worst hard-200 WAVs,
build the production-equivalent spectrogram (3840-pt FFT, freq_osr=2,
time_osr=2, ft8_lib-style Hann window with 2/nfft normalization), map
(freq_hz, dt_s) → (time_step, freq_bin, freq_sub), compute Costas
sync_score at that position, extract 79 symbols' tone magnitudes,
compute 174 max-log LLRs; encode the truth message via
`Ft8Encoder::encode_message` to derive the reference 174-bit codeword;
report mean(|LLR|), max(|LLR|), and per-bit sign-agreement with the
reference codeword. Control = same extraction at successful pancetta
decode positions.

| population | n | mean(|LLR|) | sign-agree mean | p10 | p50 | p90 | sync_score p50 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| CONTROL (production decodes) | 654 | 9.32 | 84.0% | 54.6% | **92.0%** | 100.0% | ≈ 11.5 |
| MISSED, sub-Costas (target) | 570 | 7.62 | 50.4% | 45.4% | **50.6%** | 55.7% | ≈ 0.67 |
| MISSED, all encodable | 623 | 7.57 | 50.7% | 45.4% | 50.6% | 55.7% | ≈ 1.08 |

**Kill switch (a) LLR magnitude**: gate = ≥ 10% of control. Result =
**81.7%** — PASSES handsomely. There IS energy at sub-Costas positions.

**Kill switch (b) sign-agreement**: gate = ≥ 85% median. Result =
**50.6%** — coin-flip-equivalent. FAILS catastrophically.

PROCEED requires BOTH. (b) fails by 34 percentage points. The verdict
is unambiguous: SHELVE.

## Why the LLRs are large but wrong (the structural finding)

The diagnostic surfaced an unexpected structural fact: sub-Costas
positions in busy-band hard-200 WAVs are NOT magnitude-starved. Mean
|LLR| at sub-Costas positions is 82% of control, and max |LLR| reaches
38.7 (vs control's 43.7). The energy is real.

But sign-agreement with the truth codeword sits at 50.6% — exactly what
random-noise LLRs would produce. Three things follow:

1. **The energy at sub-Costas positions is not the truth's tone energy.**
   It's energy from OTHER signals — neighboring decodes' tones leaking
   into the truth's freq_bin window, residual tones from incompletely-
   subtracted multipass decodes, broadband busy-band noise, etc. Big
   magnitudes, wrong directions.

2. **OSD's flip enumeration cannot rescue this.** OSD-2 corrects up to
   2 bits over the 91 most-reliable info positions. With ~85 of 174
   bits sign-wrong (50.6% agreement), the truth is not within 2, 3, 4,
   or any tractable flip distance of the OSD-0 hard decision. OSD's
   correction capacity is for "BP converged near the truth but missed
   the last ~2 bits", not for "the channel LLRs themselves are
   uncorrelated with the truth".

3. **The CRC-as-discriminator argument breaks down.** The hypothesis
   premised: "at noise-only positions, no 2-flip pattern produces a
   valid CRC, so CRC IS the gate." But the diagnostic shows sub-Costas
   positions aren't noise-only — they have ~80% of control's |LLR|.
   OSD-2 enumerates 4095 patterns over high-energy garbage LLRs; CRC-14
   catches 1/16384 by chance, giving ~25% chance of CRC-passing garbage
   per position. The earlier estimate of ~75 FPs per WAV at 300+ sub-
   Costas positions per WAV stands. And those FPs would have NON-trivial
   bit patterns because the LLRs are large — they'd parse as messages
   and even pass shallow plausibility checks more often than noise-only
   FPs would. The structural concern is confirmed.

implementation cost.

## Why the structural argument seemed plausible

The original framing was: "at sub-Costas positions, LLR signs are mostly
correct but some bits are wrong; OSD finds the wrong ones." The
diagnostic shows this premise is empirically false for busy-band
hard-200. The actual sub-Costas energy distribution in dense bands is
dominated by neighbor leakage and interference, not by weak versions
of the truth's own tone pattern. The truth's tone pattern at a
sub-Costas position is buried below other signals' tone patterns in
the same time-frequency neighborhood; the max-log demodulator picks the
LOUDER tone (the interferer), not the truth's tone.

A different corpus — sparse band, single-station VHF or low-activity HF
slot — might exhibit a different sub-Costas distribution: weak truths
on noise floor, not on interferer floor. There the sign-agreement might
be higher. But hard-200's wall is exactly the dense-band regime by
construction. The mechanism doesn't apply where it's needed.

## Doctrine note

The V3 SHELVE produced the methodology refinement: "geometric proximity
diagnostics need a paired decodability sub-test." hb-088 codifies this
in practice with a **per-bit sign-agreement test against the encoded
truth codeword**. This is a cheap, definitive decodability proxy for
any future mechanism that proposes to find new decodes from per-position
LLRs without going through Costas pre-gate. Recommendation: bake this
into the spec template for "find more candidates from existing
spectrogram" mechanisms.

Cost to run: ~10 seconds on top-20 hard-200 WAVs in release mode.
Information value: definitively shelved a hypothesis that would have
taken sessions 2 + 3 of plumbing + eval to disprove via production
implementation.

## Learnings

- **LLR sign-agreement is the right kill-switch for any
  per-position-LLR-mining mechanism.** Geometric proximity (V3),
  callsign coverage (hb-087), or noise-floor proxies are necessary
  but not sufficient. The direct decodability question is "do the
  channel LLR signs encode the truth?" and the answer is testable in
  isolation from BP/OSD/CRC/plausibility.

- **Sub-Costas LLR energy comes from interferers, not noise.** The
  hb-082 finding "globally relaxed sync surfaces noise" and V3's
  "bin-targeted sync surfaces noise" were under-specified — both are
  consistent with sub-Costas energy being NEIGHBOR LEAKAGE rather than
  pure noise. This explains why CRC-14 alone catches 98% of V3's false
  candidates but the remaining 2% are non-trivial: they're plausibility-
  shaped because the LLRs carry SOME structure from real signals (just
  not the truth signal at that position).

- **The hb-086 family's wall is structurally "interferer-dominated
  sub-Costas".** Any mechanism that extracts decodes from sub-Costas
  positions in dense bands must FIRST separate the truth's tone energy
  from interferer tone energy at that position. Single-position
  spectrogram-mining mechanisms (V3, hb-088) cannot do this; the
  spectrogram alone doesn't distinguish "this energy at freq_bin f0
  comes from a truth at (f0, t0)" vs "this energy comes from a truth
  at (f0+5, t0)" with leakage into f0. The natural fixes are:
  - Multi-stream separation BEFORE LLR extraction (joint decode of
    overlapping candidates as a vector problem, not a sequence of
    single-candidate decodes).
  - Higher-resolution spectrogram (more freq_osr or time_osr) that
    might disambiguate tone-aligned vs leakage energy.
  - Coherent IQ extraction at the truth's expected coordinates that
    rejects energy at adjacent freq_bins via phase-coherent matched
    filtering.
  These are different mechanism families — hb-088 closes the
  single-position-LLR-mining family on this corpus.

- **hb-087 (callsign-priors) remains the live attack on this wall.**
  hb-088's SHELVE strengthens the case for hb-087: hb-087 doesn't
  rely on the LLR sign pattern being correct, it relies on AP1/AP2
  pinning 28 bits to known values which collapses the codeword space
  and lets BP converge from a much smaller starting set. The 28-bit
  prior is independent of whether the channel LLRs encode the truth's
  tone pattern. The sibling result here doesn't change hb-087's
  decodability micro-test gate at session 2/3 — that test still must
  run — but it does mean hb-087 is the surviving candidate of the
  bypass-Costas family.

## New spawns

- Single-position spectrogram-mining family is CLOSED on hard-200.
- Future revisit conditions: a sparser corpus (rural VHF, low-activity
  HF slot) MIGHT show higher sub-Costas sign-agreement; rerun this
  diagnostic on such a corpus if it's added to the curated tier.
- The "coherent IQ matched-filter at truth coordinates" family
  (`pancetta-ft8/src/decoder.rs::extract_symbols_via_fft` or similar
  shape) is structurally different and was NOT tested here — that's a
  separate hypothesis that could be spawned independently if the wall
  warrants further attacks after hb-087 graduates or shelves.
