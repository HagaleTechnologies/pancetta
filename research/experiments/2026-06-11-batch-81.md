## Implementation

`decode_origin: Option<u8>` on ConfidenceFeatures, stamped idempotently at
the six aggregation sites in `decode_window` (commit a7309747, implemented
by subagent): 0 = primary pass, 1 = later standard pass, 2 = cross-cycle
averaging, 3 = coherent-multipass round, 4 = joint-pair retry, 5 =
a7-family (cross-correlation pass AND the fourth-pass it triggers — the
Batch 80 spec mislabeled that site; the implementer caught it), 6 = sync
relaxation. 100% of decodes stamped on both measurement corpora. 519
pancetta-ft8 tests green.

## Operating point and the honest control

CQ-population gate comparison including the v1-at-same-τ control (computed
from the dumped per-decode samples, `research/corpus/scans/batch81_samples_*.jsonl`):

| τ | hard_200: v1 / v3 FP rej | raw: v1 / v3 FP rej | recall (all) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0.35 | 11.31% / 11.31% | 3.50% / **4.90%** | 100% |
| 0.90 | 12.37% / **15.19%** | 9.79% / 9.79% | 100% |
| 0.965 | 14.13% / **15.90%** | 11.19% / **11.89%** | 100% |

The control matters: v1's min-TP score is 1.07 on both corpora, so much of
any "v3 @ high τ beats v1 @ 0.35" gain is just the threshold raise. The
origin term itself is **strictly dominant-or-equal at every τ** (never
worse, up to +2.8pp) with equal recall.

## The deliberate non-ship: the threshold lever

Raising τ to 0.90 adds +9–12pp FP rejection at 100% measured recall on
both corpora — but cuts the recall margin (distance from threshold to the
weakest real TP) from 0.72 to 0.17. That is an operator risk-posture
choice (missed-QSO cost vs phantom-TX cost), not a measurement question.
Logged in the bank under hb-103 as **OPERATOR-DECISION-PENDING**: a
one-line change (`SHIP_CONSERVATIVE` → 0.90 or a config knob) whenever
K5ARH wants the more aggressive operating point — sensible to revisit
with Phase 5 on-air experience.
