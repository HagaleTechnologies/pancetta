## What was tried

### Attempt 1 — Naive perfect-SIC α-sweep (FAILED — broken technique)

For top-50 SIC-victims:
1. Encode `neighbor.text` → 79 FT8 symbols via `Ft8Encoder`
2. Modulate at `neighbor.freq_hz` → 12.64s waveform via `Ft8Modulator`
3. Position at `dt_s` into 15s WAV (silence-pad prefix)
4. α-sweep: `residual = signal - α × shifted_wave` for
   α ∈ {0.5, 0.7, 0.9, 1.0, 1.1, 1.3, 1.5}
5. Decode residual

Result: **0/43 recovered** (7 of 50 had unencodable text).

Sanity-check revealed the failure: **at α=1.0 the subtraction did not
even kill the neighbor's own decode in 0/17 cases**. The synthesized
waveform doesn't match the real signal — subtraction is effectively
a no-op.

Diagnosis: jt9 reports `freq_hz` at 1 Hz resolution. Over a 12.64s
signal, a 0.5 Hz frequency error produces 0.5×2π×12.64 = ~39 radians
of accumulated phase drift = ~6 full cycles. The encoded waveform at
jt9-reported freq vs real signal frequency = destructive interference
at random phase across the signal. Phase-sweep (sample-level shifts)
also yielded 0/43.

**Conclusion**: this technique cannot answer the hb-218a question.
Need to use pancetta's INTERNAL SIC (which does sub-Hz frequency
refinement and coherent ML-projection).

### Why the residual is noise (mechanism)

The SIC mechanism is doing its job: coherent ML-projection removes
the strong neighbor's energy from the complex spectrogram. What
remains at the truth's expected freq/dt coords is:

1. Sub-decodable weak truth signal (∼6-15 dB below the neighbor)
2. Sidelobe spill from the subtraction (real but mostly orthogonal)
3. Wideband WAV noise

Pancetta's sync threshold is calibrated for "actual signal above
noise floor". The weak truth's signal *was* above the noise floor
before the strong neighbor occupied the same frequency cells; after
SIC, what's left is noise floor + tiny residual signal *that's
indistinguishable from noise at decodable bit-error-rate*. LDPC
on the noise converges to random codewords (CRC catches ~98% as
FPs), confirming there's no usable signal LLR information.
