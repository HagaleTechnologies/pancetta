## Why primitives, not structs

The original spec sketch had FDR take `&DecodedMessage`. But pancetta-qso does not depend on pancetta-ft8 (the latter is below it in the dep graph). FDR follows the same pattern as `CrossSequenceSeed` ⇄ `A7SeedEntry`: the public surface accepts primitives, and the coordinator translates from `pancetta_ft8::DecodedMessage` + its `ConfidenceFeatures` into `(MessageCategory, FdrFeatures)` at the call boundary. The translation table:

| `pancetta_ft8::MessageType` | `pancetta_qso::fdr::MessageCategory` |
|---|---|
| `Standard`, `Extended` | `Standard` |
| `FreeText` | `FreeText` |
| `Telemetry` | `Telemetry` |
| `DXpedition` | `DXpedition` |
| `NonStdCall` | `NonStdCall` |
| `Contest`, `FieldDay`, `RTTYRoundup` | `Contest` |
| `Unknown` | `Unknown` |

Session 4b will own this translation in the coordinator.

## Strategic implications

1. **FDR is now implementable end-to-end.** Sessions 1-3 (data ingestion) and Session 4a (gate module) together make FDR a single coordinator wiring change away from production. Session 4b is the calibration + ship decision.

2. **Placeholder thresholds are intentional.** The values shipped here (`0.10`, `0.20`, `0.25`, `0.40`) are not calibrated to anything. They're chosen to be "conservative" in the sense that they're well above the score that good-features messages produce. Session 4b will use the actual measurement to set them.

3. **The "8 of N inapplicable" measurement risk for FDR.** Recall that 8 of the implementer-thread mechanisms have been "faithful but inapplicable" on pancetta's stack. FDR may or may not graduate at Session 4b. The spec explicitly notes strong overlap with pancetta's existing `hb-103` content scoring and `is_plausible` filter; FDR may be redundant. The calibration session resolves this.

4. **Module isolation is clean.** `fdr.rs` is 350 LOC with zero pancetta-ft8 imports. The pancetta-qso ↔ pancetta-ft8 dep boundary is preserved. Any future refactor (e.g., moving `ConfidenceFeatures` to pancetta-core) is local to the coordinator.
