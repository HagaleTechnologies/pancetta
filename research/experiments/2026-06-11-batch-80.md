## The production question

The autonomous CQ gate (autonomous.rs decide()) scores candidates with
content score v1 and rejects below SHIP_CONSERVATIVE (+0.35). Does v3 at a
100%-recall-calibrated τ reject more FPs than v1 on the gate's actual
population (CQ-type decodes)?

Probe: `pancetta-research/examples/batch80_v3_operating_point.rs`
(hard_200 + raw_530 subset-200, ft8_lib truth, slot-max-normalized
decode_time_frac).

dominant on both — but:

## Why the gate does NOT flip this batch

The calibrated τ values are **not run-stable**: between two identical runs
under different CPU load, raw's 100%-recall τ moved 0.105 → 0.182 (+73%)
and hard_200's moved 0.726 → 0.666, because `decode_time_into_window` is
wall-clock. The hard_200 margin at τ* (+0.3pp) is inside that calibration
noise. Shipping a TX-gate constant calibrated on a load-sensitive feature
fails the engineering-substance bar.

The feature is a *proxy*: late decode ≈ produced by an aggressive recovery
pass (multipass residual, joint-pair retry, a7-extra). The decoder knows
that provenance **deterministically** — it just doesn't expose it. The
right fix is an ordinal `decode_origin` field on `ConfidenceFeatures`
(primary pass < later pass < cross-cycle averaging < coherent-multipass
round < joint-pair retry < a7-extra), stamped at the ~8 existing
`decode_time_into_window` emission sites. Hardware-invariant, run-stable,
and likely sharper than the timing proxy it replaces.

→ **hb-247** spawned (priority 0.55): implement decode_origin, re-run the
Batch 79 AUC and Batch 80 operating-point analyses on it, flip the gate to
v3' (origin-based) if domination holds with a stable τ.
